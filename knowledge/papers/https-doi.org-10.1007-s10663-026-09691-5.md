<summary><strong>中文摘要</strong></summary>

本文推导了斯洛文尼亚（一个欧元区小型经济体）在应对合理的暂时性外部冲击时可实施的中期财政工具路径。我们将宏观计量经济学模型SLOPOL12与随机最优控制框架相结合，其中目标函数基于对斯洛文尼亚政策制定者宏观经济优先事项的调查进行可操作化处理。利用OPTCON算法，针对基准预测及多种替代冲击情景，计算了近似最优政策。结果凸显了在稳定产出与就业、以及维持赤字与公共债务方面的财政可持续性之间存在系统性权衡。在不同情景下，同时影响需求侧和潜在产出的工具——最显著的是直接税以及对物质资本和人力资本（包括研发相关支出）的公共投资——对于周期性稳定而言是最优的；而主要具有需求侧或预算效应的工具，如增值税（VAT）、政府消费和剩余收入，则主要被用于预算整合。这些定性模式在不同冲击下均保持稳健，并阐明了在明确约束和既定政策目标下，差异化的工具有效性如何塑造政策调整的最优时机与构成。

<summary><strong>英文摘要</strong></summary>

Abstract This paper derives implementable medium-run fiscal instrument paths for Slovenia, a small Euro-Area economy, in response to plausible transitory external shocks. We combine the macroeconometric model SLOPOL12 with a stochastic optimal-control framework in which the objective function is operationalized from a survey of Slovenian policy makers’ macroeconomic priorities. Approximately optimal policies are computed using the OPTCON algorithm in relation to a baseline forecast and alternative shock scenarios. The results highlight a systematic trade-off between stabilizing output and employment and maintaining fiscal sustainability in terms of the deficit and public debt. Across scenarios, instruments that influence both the demand side and potential output, most notably direct taxes and public investment in physical and human capital (including R&amp;D-related spending), are optimal for cyclical stabilization while instruments with predominantly demand-side or budgetary effects, such as VAT, government consumption, and residual revenues, primarily assigned to budgetary consolidation. The qualitative patterns are robust across shocks and clarify how differential instrument effectiveness shapes the optimal timing and composition of policy adjustment under explicit constraints and stated policy objectives.
