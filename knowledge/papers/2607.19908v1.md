<summary><strong>中文摘要</strong></summary>

开展影响评估的核心任务在于确定谁或什么受到了干预措施的影响、何时受到影响以及影响程度如何。在地理空间情境下，这些问题可能尤为复杂，因为可能存在多种合理的暴露定义。本章引入“处理几何”（treatment geometry）作为地理空间影响评估（Geospatial Impact Evaluation, GIE）的核心概念：即数据所表征的干预措施在空间与时间上的足迹。这一足迹的定义方式将塑造识别策略及因果推断的可信度。通过借鉴涵盖空气污染、野火、森林政策、基础设施、病虫害及粮食安全等领域的文献案例，本章就如何在将现实世界干预转化为可分析数据时应对关键权衡（包括空间分辨率、时间对齐、溢出效应及边界不确定性）提供了实践指导。本章并非推荐单一最佳方法，而是为研究者提供一套诊断框架，帮助其判断在自身研究情境中哪些几何决策最为关键，并最终通过总结性问题引导读者应对这些决策。

<summary><strong>英文摘要</strong></summary>

A central task in conducting impact evaluations is determining who or what was exposed to a treatment, when, and to what degree. These questions can be especially complex in geospatial settings, where many reasonable definitions of exposure may exist. This chapter introduces treatment geometry as a core concept in geospatial impact evaluation (GIE): the spatial and temporal footprint of a treatment as represented in data. How this footprint is defined shapes identification strategies and the credibility of causal inference. Drawing on cases spanning the air pollution, wildfire, forest policy, infrastructure, pest, and food security literature, the chapter provides practical guidance on navigating key tradeoffs (including spatial resolution, temporal alignment, spillovers, and boundary uncertainty) that arise when translating real-world interventions into analyzable data. Rather than prescribing a single best approach, the chapter equips researchers with a framework for diagnosing which geometry decisions may matter most in their context, closing with synthesis questions to help readers navigate these decisions.
