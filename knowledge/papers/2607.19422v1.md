<summary><strong>中文摘要</strong></summary>

本文讨论了W. Semmler与M. Sieveking在文献\cite{SemmlerSieveking}中提出并从理论与数值角度研究的一类聚集型流动性增长模型中的极限环问题。该模型局部上属于捕食者-猎物（predator-prey）类型，其中捕食者（利润）存在同类相食（cannibalism）现象，而猎物群体（流动性）则受逻辑斯蒂（logistic）增长约束。我们对模型进行了修改，在流动性中引入弱阿利效应（weak Allee effect），并借助Hopf-Andronov理论研究了平衡点与极限环的局部分岔。本研究的主要动机源于Leijonhuvud在文献\cite{Leijonh}中提出的所谓“稳定走廊”（corridors of stability）的经济学理论。然而，这些效应的重要性同样在最初发展的生物学动态模型（尤其是数学生态学）中有所体现。

<summary><strong>英文摘要</strong></summary>

We discuss the problem of limit cycles in an aggregate-type models of liquidity growth proposed and studied both theoretically and numerically by W. Semmler and M. Sieveking in \cite{SemmlerSieveking}. Their model is locally of predator-prey type with cannibalism in predator (profit) and logistic bound on prey's population (liquidity). We modify the model to include the weak Allee effect in liquidity and to study the local bifurcations of equilibria and limit cycles by means of the Hopf-Andronov theory. The main motivation for this study follows the economic theory of the so-called corridors of stability introduced by Leijonhuvud in \cite{Leijonh}. Similar importance of these effects is, however, found also in the originally developed dynamical models in biology, especially in mathematical ecology.
