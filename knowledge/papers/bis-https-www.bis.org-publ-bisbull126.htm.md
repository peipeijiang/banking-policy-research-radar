作者：Daniel Eidan、Jon Frost、Rudraksh Kansal、Ulf Lewrick、Sang Hyuk Lim、Tomasz Rybarczyk  
尽管所有无需许可区块链（permissionless blockchain）都依赖基于代币的激励机制来维持诚实验证，但验证者奖励、协调与参与方式的结构差异，会导致去中心化、安全性与可扩展性之间出现不同的均衡状态与权衡取舍。这些权衡催生了多种第一层网络（layer 1 network）的涌现以及第二层解决方案（layer 2 solution）的扩展，进而造成跨链及链内基础设施、流动性与资产的碎片化。缓解碎片化的工具——例如跨链桥（bridge）与原生多链发行（native multi-chain issuance）——虽能减少摩擦，却重新引入了对信任、治理及运营韧性的新依赖。

<summary><strong>英文摘要</strong></summary>

by Daniel Eidan, Jon Frost, Rudraksh Kansal, Ulf Lewrick, Sang Hyuk Lim, Tomasz Rybarczyk While all permissionless blockchains use token-based incentives to sustain honest validation, differences in how validator rewards, coordination and participation are structured lead to distinct equilibria and trade-offs between decentralisation, security and scalability. These trade-offs underpin the emergence of multiple layer 1 networks and the expansion of layer 2 solutions, resulting in fragmentation of infrastructure, liquidity and assets across and within chains. Tools to mitigate fragmentation – eg bridges and native multi-chain issuance – can reduce frictions, but they reintroduce new dependencies on trust, governance and operational resilience.
