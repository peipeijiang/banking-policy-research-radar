# Sanctions Effectiveness, Development and Regime Type. Are Aid Suspensions and Economic Sanctions Alike?

[查看原文](https://ideas.repec.org/p/bfr/banfra/1042.html)

## 一句话结论

> 研究经济制裁和援助暂停对不同政权类型有效性的差异，发现富裕程度对经济制裁有保护作用但对援助暂停无效。

<summary><strong>中文摘要</strong></summary>

国际制裁在促使目标方遵循制裁发起方目标方面的有效性，一直受到国际关系（IR）与发展研究学者的关注。然而，在制裁研究中，援助暂停相较于经济制裁受到的关注较少，这可能使我们对制裁有效性的理解产生偏差。由于近期研究已证实不同类型的威权政体对制裁表现出不同程度的韧性，我们需验证此类结论是否同样适用于援助暂停。首先，我们考察不同政体类型对制裁的韧性差异，继而探究援助暂停的效应是否与一般制裁存在区别。在此基础上，我们提出假设：财富对威权政体在援助暂停中的保护作用弱于其他制裁，因为援助暂停的影响更难以规避。借助计量经济学分析，我们利用将援助暂停作为独立类别的原始数据检验假设。检验结果证实了一党制政体与君主制政体具有更强的抵抗能力。针对目标方富裕程度的最终检验揭示了一个细微差异：富裕程度增强了目标方对经济制裁的抵抗能力，但对援助暂停则无此效果。这验证了我们的规避假设：尽管替代贸易路线可抵消与特定发起方贸易禁令的影响，但替代援助方却极为罕见。

<summary><strong>英文摘要</strong></summary>

The efficacy of international sanctions in bringing about compliance with the goals of the sender is of interest to both International Relations (IR) and development scholars. Yet, aid suspensions receive less attention in sanctions research than economic sanctions, which may be biasing our understanding of sanctions efficacy. Since recent research has established that different autocratic types display diverging degrees of resilience to sanctions, we ascertain whether such claims are applicable to aid suspensions. First, we look at how resilient different regime types are to sanctions and then investigate whether results for aid suspensions differ from those for sanctions in general. After that, we hypothesise that wealth protects autocracies less from aid suspensions than from other sanctions because their effects are harder to evade. With the help of econometric analysis, we test our hypotheses on original data that feature aid suspensions as a stand-alone category. Test results corroborate the superior resistance of single-party regimes and monarchies. A final test on the role of target prosperity uncovers a nuance: affluence strengthens target resistance to economic sanctions but not to aid suspensions. This confirms our evasion hypothesis: while alternative trade routes can offset a ban on trade with a set of senders, substitute donors are rare.
