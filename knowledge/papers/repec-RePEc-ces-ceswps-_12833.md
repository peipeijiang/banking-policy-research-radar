# Do Microenterprises Maximize Profits? A Nonrandomized Vegetable-Market Experiment in India

[查看原文](https://ideas.repec.org/p/ces/ceswps/_12833.html)

<summary><strong>中文摘要</strong></summary>

我们在加尔各答蔬菜市场开展了一项非随机、市场层面的实验，对部分市场的商贩提供补贴，鼓励其销售额外农产品。扣除补贴价值后，商贩的利润提高了60%以上。然而，补贴结束后，许多商贩停止了额外农产品的销售。商贩们知晓这一盈利机会，并证明他们有能力在无援助的情况下利用该机会。我们得出结论：在考虑实际到手收入时，其行为显著偏离了利润最大化原则，这很可能是由于高边际努力成本与担心打破反竞争规范而遭受制裁的共同作用。

</details>

<details>
<summary><strong>英文摘要</strong></summary>

We ran a nonrandomized, market-level experiment in Kolkata vegetable markets in which we subsidized vendors in some markets to sell additional produce. The vendors earned over 60% higher profits, excluding the value of the subsidy. Nevertheless, after the subsidy ended many vendors stopped selling the additional produce. Vendors knew about the profitable opportunity and demonstrated that they were capable of exploiting it without assistance. We conclude that their behavior meaningfully diverges from profit maximization when considering take-home pay, likely due to a combination of high marginal costs of effort and feared sanctions from breaking anti-competitive norms.

</details>
