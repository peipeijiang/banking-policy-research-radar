<summary><strong>中文摘要</strong></summary>

非契约型商业中的企业面临一个挑战：它们难以知晓实际拥有的客户数量，因为客户可能在不告知的情况下停止购买。“购买至死”模型通过估算每位客户存活的概率（即P(alive)）来应对这一问题，该指标被用于所有主流的仪表盘、客户流失分析、客户资产及企业估值软件工具中。我们证明，这种做法混淆了两个不同的概念。在贝塔-几何分布族中，P(alive)是一个可观测的有限期重复购买概率族在无限期极限下的值。每一个有限期估计值，例如12个月内重复购买的概率，都是对可验证事件的预测。而无限期极限只能通过外推法达到，这是一种通过推测得到的客户数量。因此，隐含的客户数量仅能被部分识别：实际回头的客户构成下限，而估计惯例决定了高于该下限的报告点估计值。在一项包含31,683名客户的七年面板数据中，具有几乎相同可观测预测值的不同模型规格，对存活客户数量的估计范围从3,654到27,734不等，相差7.6倍；仅默认软件权重参数的变化就使该数量波动42%；而随后五年的购买数据从下方证伪了最大似然估计值。这一模式在CDNOW基准数据上得到复现，差异倍数为2.4倍。实践中所谓的“校准不良”大多属于类别错误：加总的P(alive)高估了实际18个月回头客数量2.25倍，而同一模型自身的18个月预测误差仅为1.18倍。解决方法是报告可审计的有限期客户数量，在指定时间范围内估计回头概率，跨评分日期和时间范围进行审计，随客户群体漂移重新校准，并在必要时以区间而非点值形式报告总客户数量。

<summary><strong>英文摘要</strong></summary>

Firms in non-contractual commerce face the challenge of knowing how many customers they actually have because customers can stop buying without ever saying they have left. Buy-Till-You-Die models address this by estimating each customer's probability of being alive, a quantity called P(alive) and used in every major software tool for dashboards, churn, customer equity, and enterprise valuation. We show this practice confounds two distinct quantities. Within the beta-geometric family, P(alive) is the infinite-horizon limit of an observable family of finite-horizon repeat-purchase probabilities. Every finite-horizon estimate, such as the probability of repeat purchase within 12 months, is a forecast of a verifiable event. The infinite-time limit can only be reached by extrapolation, a customer count obtained by dead reckoning. The implied count is therefore only partially identified: realized returners are the lower bound, and estimation conventions determine the reported point estimate above that. On a seven-year panel of 31,683 customers, specifications with nearly identical observable forecasts estimate the number of alive customers anywhere from 3,654 to 27,734, a factor of 7.6; a default software weighting parameter alone swings the count 42 percent; and five years of later purchases falsify the maximum-likelihood count from below. The patterns replicate on the CDNOW benchmark, with a 2.4x spread. Most of what practice calls miscalibration is instead a category error: summed P(alive) overshoots realized eighteen-month returners by 2.25x, while the same model's own eighteen-month forecast errs by just 1.18x. The remedy is to report an auditable horizon count, estimate return probabilities at a stated horizon, audit them across scoring dates and horizons, recalibrate as cohorts drift, and report the total count, if at all, as an interval rather than a point.
