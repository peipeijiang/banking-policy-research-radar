<summary><strong>中文摘要</strong></summary>

本文探讨了由人工智能中介的发现、排序、定价、验证及交易执行是否在货币政策传导中引入了新的摩擦。本文定义了“计算传导缺口”（Computational Transmission Gap, CTG），即经济实体在既定金融条件下潜在可获得的货币政策响应，与在考虑计算发现、资格认定、排序、验证及行动约束后实际实现的响应之间的差异。本文提出了一个“计算传导系数”，将传导效率与“计算可见性”（Computational Visibility）、“计算资格”（Computational Eligibility）、可验证性、“主体就绪度”（Agent Readiness）及“主体可行动性”（Agent Actionability）联系起来。本文区分了实体层面的衰减与总量再分配、外部泄漏以及真正未实现的经济活动，并针对计算中介市场中的需求守恒，构建了一个分阶段的核算框架。本文提出了一个分部门的、经计算增强的通胀框架，并探讨了其对通胀持续性、部门分化、产出缺口解释以及货币政策可观测性的影响。该框架被应用于欧洲房地产市场，作为高敏感性的测试市场。本文通过一个说明性且非经验性的数值模拟，考察了直接衰减、凸性排序、容量约束、国内复苏及跨境配置。本文并未声称货币政策无效或传统传导渠道已经消失，而是认为货币政策可能在金融层面仍然有效，但在计算层面变得不完整，其异质性结果取决于企业、资产及家庭的计算分配特征。本文未报告任何因果估计或经过验证的实证结果。所有提出的机制、指数及假设均需经过实证检验。《表征经济学研究计划——第十一卷，研究层20。工作论文，版本1.4。未经同行评审。》

<summary><strong>英文摘要</strong></summary>

This paper examines whether AI-mediated discovery, ranking, pricing, verification, and transaction execution introduce a new friction into monetary-policy transmission. It defines the Computational Transmission Gap (CTG) as the difference between the monetary-policy response potentially available to an economic entity under identified financial conditions and the response actually realised after computational discovery, eligibility, ranking, verification, and action constraints are taken into account. The paper proposes a computational transmission coefficient linking transmission effectiveness to Computational Visibility, Computational Eligibility, verifiability, Agent Readiness, and Agent Actionability. It distinguishes entity-level attenuation from aggregate reallocation, external leakage, and genuinely unrealised activity, developing a staged accounting framework for demand conservation in computationally mediated markets. A sectoral, computationally augmented inflation framework is proposed, with implications for inflation persistence, sectoral dispersion, output-gap interpretation, and monetary-policy observability. The framework is applied to European real estate as a high-sensitivity test market. An illustrative and non-empirical numerical simulation examines direct attenuation, convex ranking, capacity constraints, domestic recovery, and cross-border allocation. The paper does not claim that monetary policy is ineffective or that traditional transmission channels have disappeared. It argues that monetary policy may remain financially operative while becoming computationally incomplete, with heterogeneous outcomes depending on the computational allocation characteristics of firms, assets, and households. No causal estimates or validated empirical results are reported. All proposed mechanisms, indices, and hypotheses require empirical testing.
