<summary><strong>中文摘要</strong></summary>

相同的公平规则，不同的后验信念，无交易。我们研究了当卖方和买方委托人将决策权委托给接收不同价值信号并执行机器强制执行指令的代理人时的个性化定价问题。等额名义剩余规则可能不相容，因为每条规则都适用于其代理人的后验信念。在一个常见环境中，我们求解了嵌套定价、检查、指令选择以及重复关系子博弈。信号认证与执行认证具有不同效果：卖方高信号的证据可以使高价格合法化，而受约束规则的证据则可以防止未经授权的攫取。只有当验证涵盖争议命题时，它才能恢复交易并缓和双方委托人的策略。在重复交易中，超出买方参考上限的可归因报价会损耗关系资本，从而产生状态依赖的马尔可夫策略以及一个尊重参考的区域。当卖方未内化买方避免的检查成本及关系溢出效应时，验证在私人层面供给不足；但当验证有助于攫取时，则可能供给过度。该模型将人工智能测量、算法攫取与可验证约束联系起来，而无需将信任视为软件情感，或将已验证的执行视为真实价值的证明。

<summary><strong>英文摘要</strong></summary>

Same fairness rule, different posterior, no trade. We study personalized pricing when seller and buyer principals delegate to agents that receive different value signals and execute machine-enforced mandates. Equal nominal surplus rules can be incompatible because each is applied to its agent's posterior. In one common environment, we solve nested pricing, inspection, mandate-selection, and repeated-relationship subgames. Signal attestation and execution attestation have different effects: evidence of a high seller signal can legitimate a high price, whereas evidence of a restrained rule can prevent unauthorized extraction. Verification can recover trade and soften both principals' policies only when it covers the disputed proposition. In repeated transactions, attributable offers above a buyer's reference cap depreciate relationship capital, producing a state-dependent Markov policy and a reference-respecting region. Verification is privately underprovided when the seller does not internalize avoided buyer inspection and relationship spillovers, but can be overprovided when it facilitates extraction. The model links AI measurement, algorithmic extraction, and verifiable restraint without treating trust as software emotion or verified execution as proof of true value.
