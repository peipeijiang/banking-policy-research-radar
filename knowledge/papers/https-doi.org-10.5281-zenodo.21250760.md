<summary><strong>中文摘要</strong></summary>

本工作论文将拟议中的“国防、安全与韧性银行”（Defence, Security and Resilience Bank, DSRB）视为一种金融制度基础设施，而非仅仅是一个新的多边贷款机构。论文并非探讨DSRB是否为理想的政策选择，亦非试图预测其最终成败，而是聚焦于一个更为具体的制度性问题：该银行旨在履行何种金融功能，以及在长期国防、工业或宏观经济结果尚未显现之前，如何评估这一功能？论文认为，DSRB旨在解决一个在地缘政治不确定性、投资周期漫长、采购碎片化以及商业银行约束条件下可能系统性供给不足的融资环节。论文将该银行置于一个更广泛的序列中——即政治承诺如何转化为作战能力——并提出，安全-金融机构不仅应依据其创造的资本路径来评估，还应考察其在多大程度上有效约束了资源提取、不透明性、战略报告失当以及责任转移。为支撑这一分析，论文引入了SFICRA-X（安全-金融制度交叉映射与风险分配框架，涵盖提取、不透明性与纪律约束），这是一个结构化的审计与早期证据框架。SFICRA-X旨在识别制度主张、交易对手方、金融工具、风险分配、退化风险、纪律机制，以及在安全-金融机构按预期运作时最早应可观察到的证据。该框架被定位为审计与评估工具，而非预测模型或绩效评估。论文参考了加拿大政府官方公告、DSRB设计材料、银行与国防金融文献、多边开发银行治理文献及相关制度来源。由于DSRB尚未投入运营，论文将公告与设计文件视为预期制度架构的证据，而非制度绩效的证据。随附的Zenodo数据包包含最终PDF、手稿源文件、图表、方法论材料、元数据、引用文件、发布文档以及记录分析框架开发过程的转录支持材料。这些辅助材料旨在确保透明度、来源可追溯与研究可重复性，不应被解读为对手稿中事实主张的独立证据。

<summary><strong>英文摘要</strong></summary>

This working paper examines the proposed Defence, Security and Resilience Bank (DSRB) as a form of financial institutional infrastructure rather than simply as a new multilateral lending institution. Rather than asking whether the DSRB is desirable policy or attempting to predict its eventual success, the paper asks a narrower institutional question: what financial function is the Bank designed to perform, and how can that function be evaluated before long-run defence, industrial, or macroeconomic outcomes become observable? The paper argues that the DSRB is intended to address a financing step that can be systematically underprovided under conditions of geopolitical uncertainty, long investment horizons, fragmented procurement, and commercial banking constraints. It locates the Bank within a broader sequence through which political commitments become operational capability and proposes that security-finance institutions should be evaluated not only by the capital pathways they create, but also by how effectively they constrain extraction, opacity, strategic reporting, and accountability displacement. To support this analysis, the paper introduces SFICRA-X (Security-Finance Institutional Crosswalk and Risk Allocation with Extraction, Opacity, and Discipline), a structured audit and early-evidence framework. SFICRA-X is designed to identify institutional claims, counterparties, financial instruments, risk allocation, degradation risks, discipline mechanisms, and the earliest evidence that should become observable if a security-finance institution is functioning as intended. It is presented as an audit and evaluation framework, not as a predictive model or performance assessment. The paper draws on official Government of Canada announcements, DSRB design materials, banking and defence-finance literature, multilateral development bank governance literature, and related institutional sources. Because the DSRB is not yet operational, the paper treats announcements and design documents as evidence of intended institutional architecture rather than evidence of institutional performance. The accompanying Zenodo package includes the final PDF, manuscript source, figures, methodological materials, metadata, citation files, release documentation, and transcript-support materials documenting the development of the analytical framework. These supporting materials are included for transparency, provenance, and research reproducibility and should not be interpreted as independent evidence for the factual claims made in the manuscript.
