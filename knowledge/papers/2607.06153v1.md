# From Gravity to Confinement: Wealth Redistribution as Optimal Drift Design in the Fokker-Planck Framework

[查看原文](http://arxiv.org/abs/2607.06153v1)

## 一句话结论

> 该论文通过Fokker-Planck框架研究财富税设计，发现比例税不改变不平等，而累进税和转移支付能有效再分配财富，并求解最优再分配问题。

<summary><strong>中文摘要</strong></summary>

比例财富税对财富分布的作用类似于均匀引力场：它改变了福克-普朗克方程的漂移项，但不影响扩散项，从而在任意有限时间内保持基尼系数不变。这种使税收无扭曲效应的漂移-对称性，也使其无法通过市场渠道实现再分配。再分配需要打破这一对称性。累进税（约束势）用尾部更薄的分布取代了帕累托稳态，其基尼系数是累进性参数的闭式函数；源-汇项（税收资助的转移支付）则直接重塑密度分布。

我们将最优再分配表述为福克-普朗克方程的控制问题，并对包括移民、避税和投资组合扭曲在内的干预成本施加惩罚。在一般均衡中，税收设计通过总资本和生产函数产生反馈，形成具有累进性边际收益递减的自洽麦克基恩-弗拉索夫方程。福克-普朗克算子的谱间隙决定了收敛速度：累进税能在政策相关的时间尺度内实现再分配，而比例税则依赖于缓慢的人口结构更替。

<summary><strong>英文摘要</strong></summary>

A proportional wealth tax acts as a uniform gravitational field on the wealth distribution: it shifts the drift of the Fokker-Planck equation without altering the diffusion, preserving the Gini coefficient at all finite times. The same drift-shift symmetry that makes the tax non-distortionary also makes it non-redistributive through the market channel. Redistribution requires breaking this symmetry. A progressive tax (confining potential) replaces the Pareto steady state with a thinner-tailed distribution whose Gini is a closed-form function of the progressivity parameter; source-sink terms (tax-funded transfers) reshape the density directly.
  We formulate optimal redistribution as a control problem for the Fokker-Planck equation, penalising intervention costs including migration, evasion, and portfolio distortion. In general equilibrium the tax design feeds back through aggregate capital and the production function, yielding a self-consistent McKean-Vlasov equation with diminishing returns to progressivity. The spectral gap of the Fokker-Planck operator determines convergence speed: progressive taxes redistribute within policy-relevant timescales, whereas proportional taxes rely on slow demographic turnover.
