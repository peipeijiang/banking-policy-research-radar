<summary><strong>中文摘要</strong></summary>

近期价格冲击引发了关于遏制欧洲对化石天然气进口依赖的呼声，但这一目标的成本及其所能提供的消费者保护仍不确定。本文通过在欧洲能源系统模型中对化石天然气供应施加约束来填补这一研究空白，该模型以高时空分辨率对所有天然气用途的减排进行协同优化。减少进口依赖在经济上具有吸引力：通过发电以及工业和建筑领域的低温热力节约，欧洲可以将其天然气消费量减半，每年节省160亿欧元，使需求与欧洲大陆2000亿立方米的产能相匹配。这一额外系统成本与消费者目前因天然气进口价格上涨2欧元/兆瓦时所产生的支出相当。然而，仅凭这种自主性并不能保护消费者免受全球天然气价格波动的影响：我们发现，即使天然气在能源结构中的占比很小，它仍在决定边际电价方面占据主导地位，这使得消费者在没有额外政策措施的情况下仍然面临风险。

<summary><strong>英文摘要</strong></summary>

Recent price shocks have prompted calls to curb Europe's dependence on fossil gas imports, but the cost of this goal, and the consumer protection it affords, remain uncertain. Here we address this gap by imposing constraints on fossil gas supply in a European energy system model that co-optimises abatement across all gas uses at high spatio-temporal resolution. Cutting import reliance proves economically compelling: through savings in power generation and low-temperature heat in industry and buildings, Europe can halve its natural gas consumption for 16bnEUR/a, aligning demand with the continent's production capacity of 200 bcm. This extra system cost is comparable to what consumers spend today on a 2 EUR/MWh rise in gas import prices. However, this sovereignty alone does not shield consumers from global gas price volatility: we find that, even at a small share of the mix, gas remains dominant in shaping the marginal electricity price, leaving consumers exposed without additional policy measures.
