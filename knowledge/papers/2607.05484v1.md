<summary><strong>中文摘要</strong></summary>

采用非参数机器学习模型进行监管资本估算引发了一个根本性的治理挑战：无法以监管机构可审计的方式解释模型输出。这一“黑箱”问题仍是高斯过程回归（Gaussian Process Regression, GPR）及相关机器学习架构在内部资本充足评估程序（ICAAP）和全面资本分析与审查（CCAR）工作流中应用的主要障碍，尽管这些模型在预测能力上优于传统参数化方法。

本文通过SHARC（用于监管资本的SHAP解释框架）这一可解释性框架来应对该障碍，该框架针对混合GPR-HS架构及其压力测试扩展而设计。基于合作博弈论、满足局部准确性、缺失性、一致性和效率性等性质的Shapley加法解释（SHapley Additive exPlanations, SHAP）被应用于三种宏观情景下的压力风险价值（Stressed Value-at-Risk, SVaR）输出：西亚战争、气候风险以及人工智能泡沫/监管负担。

SHARC将SVaR分解为基准成分、均值驱动成分和波动率驱动成分，从而在情景设计与资本结果之间建立透明关联。研究得出两项发现。第一，SHARC始终将非线性SVaR输出与底层情景输入相关联，证实了框架的保真度，并为资本驱动因素提供了可审计的追溯性。第二，在压力条件下，均值回报成分（方向性损失幅度）在决定资本水平方面主导方差成分（波动率基准），这对资本限额设定、头寸管理及对冲策略具有重要影响。研究结果表明，SHARC构建了一个与监管要求一致的可解释性层，使混合GPR-HS框架完全可审计，并符合交易账簿基本审查（FRTB）、ICAAP第二支柱及CCAR透明度要求。

<summary><strong>英文摘要</strong></summary>

The adoption of non-parametric machine learning models for regulatory capital estimation introduces a fundamental governance challenge: the inability to explain model outputs in a manner auditable by supervisory bodies. This 'black box' problem remains a major barrier to the adoption of Gaussian Process Regression (GPR) and related ML architectures in ICAAP and CCAR workflows despite their predictive advantages over traditional parametric approaches.
  This paper addresses this barrier through SHARC (SHAP for Regulatory Capital), an explainability framework for the Hybrid GPR-HS architecture and its stress-testing extension. SHapley Additive exPlanations (SHAP), derived from cooperative game theory and satisfying the properties of Local Accuracy, Missingness, Consistency, and Efficiency, are applied to Stressed Value-at-Risk (SVaR) outputs under three macro scenarios: West Asia War, Climate Risk, and AI Bubble/Regulatory Burden.
  SHARC decomposes SVaR into baseline, mean-driven, and volatility-driven components, enabling transparent linkage between scenario design and capital outcomes. Two findings emerge. First, SHARC consistently links non-linear SVaR outputs to underlying scenario inputs, confirming framework fidelity and providing auditable traceability of capital drivers. Second, under stress conditions, the mean return component (directional loss magnitude) dominates the variance component (volatility baseline) in determining capital levels, with implications for capital limit-setting, position management, and hedging strategy. The results establish SHARC as a regulator-aligned explainability layer that makes the Hybrid GPR-HS framework fully auditable and consistent with FRTB, ICAAP Pillar 2, and CCAR transparency requirements.
