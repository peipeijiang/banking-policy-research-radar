<summary><strong>中文摘要</strong></summary>

商业银行在竞争激烈且以客户为导向的环境中运营，一线员工在日常互动中塑造着服务质量感知。尽管银行在员工培训方面的能力建设投入巨大，但许多银行仍面临与响应性、沟通、投诉处理及服务一致性相关的投诉。本研究探讨了商业银行一线员工培训能力建设与服务品质行为之间的关系，并考察了员工敬业度的调节作用。基于人力资本理论、工作需求-资源模型及社会交换理论，本研究采用纵向调查设计，以斯里兰卡一家领先商业银行的一线员工为研究对象。最初通过培训前与培训后调查收集了150名一线员工的数据。在主题专家（Subject Matter Expert, SME）的参与下，设计了一项为期两天的专项能力建设干预措施，并依据柯克帕特里克（Kirkpatrick）的学习与行为评估视角，通过间隔一个月的培训前与培训后调查进行评估。能力建设通过产品知识、多任务处理、问题解决、服务及时性、社交礼仪及个人仪表等维度进行操作化。员工敬业度采用乌得勒支工作投入量表（Utrecht Work Engagement Scale, UWES-17）进行测量，而服务品质行为则采用基于SERVQUAL模型改编的行为测量工具，并辅以反映实际银行情境的关键事件场景。描述性统计与配对样本t检验使用SPSS软件，偏最小二乘结构方程模型（PLS-SEM）分析则使用SmartPLS软件。研究结果显示，产品知识、多任务处理、问题解决及社交礼仪显著影响服务品质行为。员工敬业度强化了若干能力-行为关系，尤其是涉及产品知识、问题解决、服务及时性及社交礼仪的关系。本研究对培训迁移、敬业度及服务品质相关文献的贡献在于，揭示了在要求严苛的服务环境中，若员工未能在心理上投入以应用所获能力，仅靠能力开发本身是不够的。

<summary><strong>英文摘要</strong></summary>

Commercial banks operate in competitive and customer-driven environments where frontline employees shape service-quality perceptions during daily interactions. Despite substantial investments in competency building in terms of employee training, many banks continue to face complaints relating to responsiveness, communication, complaint handling, and service consistency. This study examines the relationship between competency building in terms of training and service-quality behavior among frontline employees in commercial banking and investigates the moderating role of employee engagement. Drawing on Human Capital Theory, the Job Demands-Resources model, and Social Exchange Theory, the study adopted a longitudinal survey design involving frontline employees of a leading commercial bank in Sri Lanka. Data were initially collected from 150 frontline employees using pre-training and post-training surveys. A two-day specially designed competency-building intervention was developed using Subject Matter Expert input and evaluated through pre-training and post-training surveys administered one month apart, consistent with Kirkpatrick’s learning and behavioral evaluation perspectives. Competency building was operationalized through product knowledge, multitasking, problem solving, timeliness in serving, social etiquette, and personal appearance. Employee engagement was measured using the Utrecht Work Engagement Scale (17), while service-quality behavior was measured using an adapted SERVQUAL-based behavioral instrument supported by critical incident scenarios reflecting actual banking situations. SPSS was used for descriptive statistics and paired sample t-tests, while SmartPLS was used for PLS-SEM analysis. The findings revealed that product knowledge, multitasking, problem solving, and social etiquette significantly influenced service-quality behavior. Employee engagement strengthened several competency-behavior relationships, particularly those involving product knowledge, problem solving, timeliness, and social etiquette. The study contributes to training-transfer, engagement, and service-quality literature by showing that competency development alone is insufficient unless employees are psychologically engaged to apply acquired competencies in demanding service environments.
