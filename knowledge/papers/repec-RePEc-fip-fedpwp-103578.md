<summary><strong>中文摘要</strong></summary>

Baumeister与Hamilton（2018）对实证宏观经济学领域近100项重要研究的结果提出了质疑。我们证明，在Haar测度下的均匀先验分布中，他们对典型关注问题的分布不对称性的担忧，实际上源于一种未被承认的符号约束。我们还表明，这种先验分布会在简化式参数条件下对单个脉冲响应产生对称的先验分布，或者更一般地，当简化式协方差矩阵的先验分布排除了残差间的相关性时（如明尼苏达先验的典型实现方式），也会产生对称先验分布。此外，我们提出了一套理论以避免Baumeister与Hamilton批评中的陷阱。该理论的核心是一个命题，该命题确立了任何约束均可分解为三类：尺度约束、标签约束和经济约束。我们利用这一理论开发了一种基于单位模归一化的推断算法，以解决贝叶斯SVAR方法使用者普遍面临的实际问题。

<summary><strong>英文摘要</strong></summary>

The results of nearly 100 prominent studies in empirical macroeconomics have been called into question by Baumeister and Hamilton (2018). We show that their concern about distributional asymmetry for a typical question of interest under a uniform prior with respect to the Haar measure is actually driven by an unacknowledged sign restriction. We also demonstrate that such a prior induces symmetric prior distributions over individual impulse responses conditional on the reduced-form parameters, or more generally when the prior over the reduced-form covariance matrix rules out correlation among the residuals, as in the typical implementation of the Minnesota prior. Furthermore, we provide a theory for avoiding the pitfalls of Baumeister and Hamilton’s critique. Key to our theory is a proposition establishing that any restriction can be decomposed into three types: scale, label, and economic. We use this theory to develop an algorithm for inference based on the unit modulus normalization that tackles a practical problem commonly faced by users of Bayesian SVAR methods.
