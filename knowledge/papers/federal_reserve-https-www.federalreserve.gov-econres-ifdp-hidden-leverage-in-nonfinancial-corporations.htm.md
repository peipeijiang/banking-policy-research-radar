<summary><strong>中文摘要</strong></summary>

科迪·卡伦：相当一部分企业债务仍隐藏在资产负债表之外。我记录了非金融企业中两种表外杠杆形式：经营租赁（2019年前）和期内借款——即在报告期内发行并偿还的短期债务，我是首位在非金融企业中研究这一现象的人。约29%的上市公司使用了大量经营租赁，12%的公司有大量期内借款的证据，其中不成比例的子集同时使用了这两种隐性债务。使用大量隐性杠杆的公司通常规模较小，更依赖短期融资，受到成熟市场参与者的监督较少，且报告的杠杆率较低，这表明它们利用表外债务来营造虚假的杠杆状况。当2019年的会计变更揭示了大量经营租赁后，受影响的公司随后削减了25%的资本支出和14%的研发支出，面临更高的高管更替风险和利益相关者审查，并出现更频繁的会计问题。关键的是，大量经营租赁的披露导致这些被曝光的公司减少了期内借款，并提高了其报告的非租赁杠杆。

<summary><strong>英文摘要</strong></summary>

Cody Kallen A substantial portion of corporate debt remains hidden from balance sheets. I document two forms of off-balance-sheet leverage in nonfinancial corporations: operating leases (pre-2019) and intraperiod borrowing—short-term debt issued and repaid within reporting periods, which I am the first to study in nonfinancial firms. Approximately 29 percent of publicly traded firms used substantial operating leases and 12 percent show evidence of substantial intra-period borrowing, with a disproportionate subset using both types of hidden debt. Firms using substantial hidden leverage are generally smaller, more reliant on short-term funding, are less monitored by sophisticated market participants, and report lower leverage, suggesting they use off-balance-sheet debt to project false leverage profiles. When accounting changes in 2019 revealed substantial operating leases, affected firms subsequently cut capital expenditures by 25 percent and R&D by 14 percent, faced heightened risks of executive turnover and stakeholder scrutiny, and experienced more frequent accounting problems. Critically, revelation of substantial operating leases caused these exposed firms to curtail their intra-period borrowing and to raise their reported non-lease leverage.
