<summary><strong>中文摘要</strong></summary>

本文研究“横向复杂性”（horizontal complexity）——即相似任务所需行动的变化——在组织设计中的作用。一个连续统的工人各自选择行动以适应遵循高斯过程（Gaussian process）的局部状态。总部可以将工人分组为“团队”（teams），从而简化其在协调整个组织时所面临的注意力问题，但代价是抑制了适应性。利用谱理论（spectral theory）的工具，本文揭示了横向复杂性如何影响团队规模：大致而言，总部未解决的变化会扩大团队规模，而总部通过注意力吸收的、集中于某些维度的变化则会缩小团队规模。

</details>

<details>
<summary><strong>英文摘要</strong></summary>

I study the role of \emph{horizontal complexity} -- defined as the variation in actions that similar tasks require -- in organization design. A continuum of workers each choose an action to adapt to a local state that follows a Gaussian process across locations. Headquarters can group workers into \emph{teams}, simplifying the attention problem it faces in coordinating across the organization, at the cost of inhibiting adaptation. Tools from spectral theory identify how horizontal complexity affects team size: roughly speaking, variation left unresolved by headquarters expands teams, while variation concentrated along dimensions that headquarters absorbs through attention shrinks them.
