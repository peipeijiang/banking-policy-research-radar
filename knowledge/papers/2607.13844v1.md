<summary><strong>中文摘要</strong></summary>

本文研究更廉价的人工智能辅助研究如何改变认证科学的机构。人工智能降低制作精良手稿成本的速度，快于其降低判断底层贡献是否具有价值的成本。因此，文稿的精致度（Polish）失去信息价值，进入者数量扩大，未认证研究池的平均质量可能下降。在固定标准下，由于外部选项恶化，对可信认证的支付意愿随之上升。拥有市场力量的认证机构能够获取这一溢价；竞争与替代性披露规则未必产生相同的费用。在固定评审能力与弱承诺条件下，认证反而被稀释。在该扩展情形中，对投稿征收的部分庇古税（Pigouvian toll）以及评审能力的影子价值均随人工智能辅助的进入而上升。本文的贡献在于将人工智能经济学与科学生产同信号传递、认证及同行评审联系起来：更廉价的生产将稀缺性向下游转移——从使研究看起来可信，转向验证哪些研究确实可信。

<summary><strong>英文摘要</strong></summary>

I study how cheaper AI-assisted research changes the institutions that certify science. AI lowers the cost of producing a polished manuscript faster than it lowers the cost of judging whether the underlying contribution is valuable. Polish therefore loses information, entry expands and the average quality of the uncertified pool can fall. At a fixed standard, the willingness to pay for credible certification then rises because the outside option deteriorates. A certifier with market power can capture this premium; competition and alternative disclosure rules need not produce the same fee. With fixed review capacity and weak commitment, certification instead dilutes. In that extension, the partial Pigouvian toll on submissions and the shadow value of review capacity both rise with AI-assisted entry. The contribution is to connect the economics of AI and scientific production to signaling, certification and peer review: cheaper production shifts scarcity downstream, from making research look credible to verifying which research is credible.
