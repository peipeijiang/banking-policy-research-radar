<summary><strong>英文摘要</strong></summary>

This paper synthesizes the Pconf(Conformal Maintenance Power) paradigm, a novel theoretical framework that redefines the structural stability of microscopic bound systems in an expanding universe as an active, continuous thermodynamic process rather than a passive geometric state. The framework establishes a universal dissipation limit Pconf=32ℏH2Pconf=23ℏH2—the fundamental thermodynamic cost required for quantum systems to maintain their physical scales against cosmic expansion. The paper reviews seven foundational and applied studies spanning extended electrodynamics, quantum thermodynamics, emergent gravity, core-collapse supernovae, dark energy emergence, fine-structure constant evolution, and inflationary gauge field dynamics. Four critical theoretical challenges are rigorously addressed: (1) the paradox of emergent gravity in a static universe, (2) the contradiction of effective Hubble screening, (3) the scale-dependence of defining bound systems, and (4) the potential over-damping of primordial fluctuations during inflation. Through the application of local vacuum flow dynamics, hierarchical gauge separation, the renormalization group approach, and the fluctuation-dissipation theorem, these apparent paradoxes are shown to be mathematically consistent gateways to a deeper unification of general relativity and quantum mechanics. The paradigm provides first-principles resolutions to long-standing cosmological tensions including the cosmological constant problem, the cosmic coincidence problem, the Hubble tension, the spatial dipole of the fine-structure constant, the primordial magnetic field generation problem, and the supernova shock revival problem, establishing a comprehensive, falsifiable foundation for next-generation physics.
