# Trust by Context, Not by Design? A Quantitative Study of Data Donation Willingness for Open-Source Civic AI in Switzerland

[查看原文](http://arxiv.org/abs/2607.21044v1)

<summary><strong>中文摘要</strong></summary>

公民人工智能系统日益支持民主参与，然而与之互动可能暴露敏感的政治观点，从而在改进AI模型与居民对隐私和同意的期望之间产生张力。本研究考察了透明度和用户控制的条件，在此条件下瑞士居民愿意捐赠其匿名化的聊天机器人对话，以训练一个开源AI模型。一项2×2受试者间因子设计评估了数据营养标签（Data Nutrition Label）和细粒度同意控制面板（granular consent dashboard）如何影响捐赠决策。该实验通过一项多语言在线调查进行，其中包含一个由Apertus-70B模型驱动的定制聊天机器人。对205名参与者的分析显示，透明度与控制均未显著影响捐赠行为。捐赠率普遍较高（总体为91.7%），产生了天花板效应，贝叶斯检验确认了处理效应不存在。控制面板提升了感知控制感，但未提高捐赠率，且高控制组参与者主动限制了其数据使用设置。对120份开放式回答的定性分析表明，居民将捐赠视为对公共利益的贡献，其动机源于民主参与、开源模型及研究，同时许多人认为其匿名化查询不具个人属性，因此风险较低。通过隐私计算（privacy calculus）进行解读，在高制度信任下，高感知收益与低感知风险并存，因此权衡双方趋于一致，界面设计的影响力有限。提供控制的作用更多是让居民定义其贡献的条件，而非提高捐赠率。

<summary><strong>英文摘要</strong></summary>

Civic AI systems increasingly support democratic participation, yet interactions with them may reveal sensitive political views, creating tension between improving AI models and residents' expectations of privacy and consent. This study examines the conditions of transparency and user control under which Swiss residents are willing to donate their anonymized chatbot conversations to train an open-source AI model. A 2x2 between-subjects factorial design evaluated how a Data Nutrition Label and a granular consent dashboard influence donation decisions. The experiment was delivered via a multilingual online survey featuring a custom chatbot powered by the Apertus-70B model. Analysis of the 205 participants revealed that neither transparency nor control significantly affected donation behavior. Rates were uniformly high (91.7% overall), producing a ceiling effect, and Bayesian checks confirmed the absence of treatment effects. The dashboard raised perceived control but not donation, and high-control participants actively restricted their data-use settings. A qualitative analysis of 120 open-ended responses indicates that residents framed donation as a contribution to the public good, motivated by democratic participation, an open-source model, and research, while many regarded their anonymized queries as non-personal and therefore low in risk. Interpreted through the privacy calculus, a high perceived benefit coincided with a low perceived risk under high institutional trust, so both sides of the trade-off aligned and interface design had little leverage. Offering control served less to raise donation than to let residents define the terms of their contribution.
