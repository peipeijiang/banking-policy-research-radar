<summary><strong>中文摘要</strong></summary>

投资者在国债拍卖中的行为与信息的处理及估值密切相关。本研究探讨了2017年8月至2025年12月亚美尼亚主权债务市场背景下投资者与信息相关的行为。亚美尼亚的金融市场规模较小，但相对较深，且仅涉及少数投资者。这一特殊情况为检验理性疏忽理论（rational inattention theory）的适用性提供了条件。尽管小型开放经济中的信息可能十分丰富，但这并不意味着信息的估值方式相同。小型开放经济中的投资者将注意力集中于监测一些显著的政策变量，包括中央银行政策利率（central bank policy interest rate）和总体通胀率（headline inflation），但忽略了诸如需求动态等更为具体的信号。我们提出，拍卖中的截止收益率（cut-off yield）与加权平均收益率（weighted average yield）之间的利差可作为信息疏忽（information inattention）的衡量指标。根据理性疏忽理论，投资者会策略性地分配注意力，并聚焦于那些易于获取且公开的信号。因此，我们的假设是，拍卖利差与部分信息处理（partial information processing）相一致，即需求信号相对于政策利率被低估。事实上，分析表明，截止收益率仍与政策利率相关，而利差并未扩大。这与收益率利差反映注意力分配中的有限理性（bounded rationality）的假设一致。在政府借贷需求增加的时期，拍卖成为信号传递的关键来源，因此有必要对其进行研究。

<summary><strong>英文摘要</strong></summary>

Investors’ behavior in the auctions for government bonds is closely associated with the processing and valuing of information. This study explores investors’ behavior in relation to information in the context of the sovereign debt market in Armenia in August 2017 to December 2025. Armenia has a small financial market, which is relatively deep and involves only a small number of investors. This particular situation allows for testing the applicability of the rational inattention theory. While information in a small open economy can be abundant, it does not follow that information is valued in the same way. Investors in a small open economy focus their attention on monitoring some salient policy variables, including the central bank policy interest rate and headline inflation but ignore some more specific signals such as demand dynamics. We suggest that the spread between the cut-off yield and the weighted average yield in the auction can be used as a measure of information inattention. According to the rational inattention theory, investors allocate their attention strategically and focus on those signals that can be obtained easily and publicly. Therefore, our hypothesis is that the auction spread is consistent with partial information processing, whereby demand signals are underweighted relative to the policy rate. Indeed, the analysis suggests that the cut-off yield remains correlated with the policy rate, whereas the spread does not increase. This is consistent with the hypothesis that yield spreads reflect bounded rationality in attention allocation. During the periods of increased need for government borrowing, auctions become the key sources of signaling and thus need to be studied.
