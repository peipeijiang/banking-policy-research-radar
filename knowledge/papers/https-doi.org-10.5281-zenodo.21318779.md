本文将共形维持功率（\(P_{\text{conf}} = \frac{3}{2}\hbar H^2\)）的概念——该概念源自广义相对论与量子热力学的统一——拓展至早期宇宙暴胀时期的极端环境。基于我们前期工作所建立的基础理论框架：（1）《膨胀时空中的微束缚系统能量耗散与尺度维持：一种扩展的麦克斯韦方法》\cite{kurokawa2026a}，该文引入了微束缚系统为抵抗哈勃流（Hubble flow）以维持其静态尺度所需的连续能量耗散这一基本概念；（2）《膨胀时空中微束缚系统普适耗散极限的三条路径》\cite{kurokawa2026b}，该文确立了宇宙膨胀中量子系统耗散率的普适热力学界限；（3）《引力的热力学起源：膨胀时空中量子束缚系统的共形维持》\cite{kurokawa2026c}，该文论证了共形维持与引力效应涌现之间的深层联系；以及（4）《引力坍缩中共形维持电流的几何能量注入：解决核坍缩超新星激波复活问题的新途径》\cite{kurokawa2026d}，该文将共形维持形式应用于极端天体物理环境——本研究从第一性原理出发，系统阐述了时空几何形变对局域规范场施加的热力学耗散动力学。在暴胀时期，当哈勃参数\(H\)取极大正值（\(H_{\text{inf}} \sim 10^{14}\) GeV）时，几何膨胀对局域系统起到“强摩擦”（能量耗散至真空）的作用。本文数学证明，通过考虑系统试图维持其共形尺度的热力学约束，极端哈勃膨胀充当了一个主动环境，对局域系统施加失控的几何摩擦。本文展示了在这种极端几何摩擦下，规范场的共形不变性如何不可避免地破缺，以及相变动力学如何被改变。该理论框架在不引入任何特设假设的前提下，为现代宇宙学面临的多个关键问题提供了统一且自然的解答：（1）通过共形维持电流（Conformal Maintenance Current, \(J_{\text{conf}}\)）从第一性原理产生原初磁场，该电流不可避免地破缺共形不变性，并通过几何过阻尼将磁场冻结于空间；（2）通过“几何淬灭效应”（geometric quench effect）抑制拓扑缺陷（磁单极、宇宙弦），该效应显著延迟相变并抑制缺陷产生率；以及（3）通过宇宙焦耳加热（cosmological Joule heating）从第一性原理解释再加热与熵产生，其中物质在膨胀时空中为维持自身尺度而承受的几何摩擦热，构成了热大爆炸的基本热源。本文得出结论，暴胀是一个极端的“宇宙热机”，并讨论了在宇宙微波背景B模式偏振与原初引力波谱中探测几何耗散信号的前景。

<summary><strong>英文摘要</strong></summary>

This paper extends the concept of the Conformal Maintenance Power (Pconf=32ℏH2Pconf=23ℏH2), derived from the unification of general relativity and quantum thermodynamics, to the extreme environment of the inflationary epoch in the early universe. Building upon the foundational theoretical framework established in our previous works—(1) "Energy Dissipation and Scale Maintenance of Micro-Bound Systems in Expanding Spacetime: An Extended Maxwellian Approach" \cite{kurokawa2026a}, which introduced the fundamental concept of continuous energy dissipation required for microscopic bound systems to maintain their static scales against the Hubble flow; (2) "Three Paths to the Universal Dissipation Limit of Microscopic Bound Systems in Expanding Spacetime" \cite{kurokawa2026b}, which established the universal thermodynamic bounds on dissipation rates for quantum systems in cosmic expansion; (3) "The Thermodynamic Origin of Gravity: Conformal Maintenance of Quantum Bound Systems in Expanding Spacetime" \cite{kurokawa2026c}, which demonstrated the deep connection between conformal maintenance and the emergence of gravitational effects; and (4) "Geometric Energy Injection via Conformal Maintenance Current in Gravitational Collapse: A Novel Approach to the Core-Collapse Supernova Shock Revival Problem" \cite{kurokawa2026d}, which applied the conformal maintenance formalism to extreme astrophysical environments—this study formulates from first principles the thermodynamic dissipation dynamics that the geometric deformation of spacetime imposes on local gauge fields. During the inflationary epoch, when the Hubble parameter HH assumes enormous positive values (Hinf∼1014Hinf∼1014 GeV), geometric expansion functions as an ``intense friction'' (energy dissipation into the vacuum) for local systems. This study mathematically proves that by considering the thermodynamic constraint of a system attempting to maintain its conformal scale, extreme Hubble expansion functions as an active environment imposing runaway geometric friction on local systems. The paper demonstrates how, under this extreme geometric friction, the conformal invariance of gauge fields is inevitably broken and how phase transition dynamics are transformed. The theoretical framework presents unified and natural solutions, without adding any ad hoc assumptions, to multiple critical problems faced by modern cosmology: (1) The first-principles generation of primordial magnetic fields through the Conformal Maintenance Current JconfJconf that inevitably breaks conformal invariance and freezes magnetic fields in space via geometric overdamping; (2) The suppression of topological defects (monopoles, cosmic strings) through a geometric quench effect'' that dramatically delays phase transitions and suppresses defect generation rates; and (3) The first-principles explanation of reheating and entropy generation through cosmological Joule heating, where the geometric frictional heat resulting from matter's struggle to maintain its scale in expanding spacetime serves as the fundamental heat source for the hot Big Bang. The paper concludes that inflation was an extremecosmological heat engine'' and discusses future observational prospects for detecting geometric dissipation signatures in the cosmic microwave background B-mode polarization and primordial gravitational wave spectra.
