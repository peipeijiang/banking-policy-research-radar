<summary><strong>中文摘要</strong></summary>

网络保险市场的扩张仍面临累积事件（accumulation events）的威胁，这类事件可能同时影响大量保单持有人。尽管除2017年WannaCry和NotPetya等全球性网络攻击外，迄今此类灾难性事件鲜有发生，但网络风险的本质使其发生具有现实可能性。因此，需要借助压力测试工具（stress-testing tools）来评估保险组合能否抵御此类危机。基于此，欧洲保险与职业养老金管理局（EIOPA）已将云服务中断（cloud outage）列为网络保险压力测试框架中需重点考量的关键场景之一。本文提出了一种对云服务中断场景进行建模与校准的框架，并用于衡量网络保险组合的分散化效应（diversification）。同时，我们展示了这种分散化如何防范累积风险，并提供了承保指引（underwriting guidelines），以降低保险组合在云服务中断场景中的脆弱性。

<summary><strong>英文摘要</strong></summary>

The expansion of the cyber insurance market remains exposed to the threat of accumulation events that could simultaneously affect a large number of policyholders. Although few such catastrophes have been observed so far, apart from worldwide cyberattacks such as WannaCry and NotPetya in 2017, the nature of cyber risk makes their occurrence plausible. Stress-testing tools are therefore needed to assess whether an insurance portfolio can withstand such crises. In this perspective, the European Insurance and Occupational Pensions Authority (EIOPA) has identified cloud outage as one of the key scenarios to consider in cyber insurance stress-testing frameworks. In this paper, we propose a framework to model and calibrate cloud-outage scenarios and to measure the diversification of a cyber insurance portfolio. We also show how this diversification can protect against accumulation risk and provide underwriting guidelines to reduce the vulnerability of a portfolio to cloud-outage scenarios.
