# Measuring Consumption with Credit Card Data: Benchmarking and Beyond

<summary><strong>中文摘要</strong></summary>

我们构建了一个新颖的月度县级消费数据集，该数据集源自美联储Y-14M报告中超过3.5亿张信用卡的支出数据，覆盖了自2014年以来美国3000多个县。我们首先证明，该数据能够紧密近似传统消费指标：在全国层面，它解释了月度调整后个人消费支出（PCE）增长变动的92%；在州层面，它捕捉了年度调整后PCE增长中有意义的横截面差异。作为概念验证，我们利用该县级月度面板数据，估算了不同县级收入分布群体对货币政策冲击的异质性消费反应——这一分析在传统消费数据下无法实现。研究发现，低收入县的支出下降幅度大于高收入县，这与异质性代理人新凯恩斯主义模型（Heterogeneous Agent New Keynesian models）的结论一致。最后，我们为使用类似数据的研究人员提供实践指导，讨论了数据覆盖范围、样本构成以及如何从征信局数据中近似估算信用卡支出。

<summary><strong>英文摘要</strong></summary>

We introduce a novel monthly county-level consumption dataset constructed from spending data on over 350 million credit cards in the Federal Reserve's Y-14M reports, covering over 3,000 U.S. counties since 2014. We first show that the data closely approximate traditional consumption measures, explaining 92 percent of the variation in monthly adjusted personal consumption expenditures (PCE) growth at the national level and capturing meaningful cross-sectional variation in annual adjusted PCE growth at the state level. As a proof of concept, we use the county-month panel to estimate heterogeneous consumption responses to monetary policy shocks across the county-level income distribution, an analysis infeasible with traditional consumption data. We find that low-income counties exhibit larger spending declines than high-income counties, consistent with heterogeneous agent New Keynesian models. Finally, we provide practical guidance for researchers working with similar data, discussing coverage, sample composition, and the approximation of credit card spending from credit bureau data.

</details>
