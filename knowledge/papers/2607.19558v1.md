<summary><strong>中文摘要</strong></summary>

我们研究一个面临买方的垄断者，该买方的估值由交易前投资决定。在定价之前，卖方会观测到关于买方私人投资成本的一个信号（买方画像）。有助于卖方攫取剩余的信息也可能削弱买方创造剩余的激励。我们刻画了在所有可能的画像下买方与卖方所能实现的收益。在帕累托边界上，若投资增加，敲竹杠风险总是使卖方获得的收益增长速度快于投资所创造的剩余增长速度。因此，保护买方福利需要抑制投资，即便投资在社会效率上是有效的。

</details>

<details>
<summary><strong>英文摘要</strong></summary>

We study a monopolist facing a buyer whose valuation is determined by pre-trade investment. Before setting price, the seller observes a signal about the buyer's private investment cost (buyer profiling). Information that helps the seller extract surplus can also undermine the buyer's incentive to create it. We characterize the buyer-seller payoffs attainable across all possible profiling. On the Pareto frontier, if investment increases, hold-up risk always raises the payoff that the seller captures faster than the surplus that the investment creates. Protecting buyer welfare therefore requires discouraging investment, even though investment is socially efficient.

</details>
