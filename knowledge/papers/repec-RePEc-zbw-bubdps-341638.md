# Bivariate sudden stop analysis of equity and bond fund flows to emerging markets using isolation forest

<summary><strong>中文摘要</strong></summary>

本文应用机器学习方法与异常检测技术，对组合资本流动的骤停现象进行分析。采用孤立森林（Isolation Forest）方法，生成了股票与债券基金流向新兴市场的单变量及双变量骤停指标，并提供了异常评分与异常分类。研究结果表明，近年来流向新兴市场的异常组合资本流动有所增加。此外，相较于传统的骤停分析方法，孤立森林方法在识别与近期资本流动波动相关的异常事件方面表现更优，这些波动包括新冠疫情爆发以及近年来发达经济体利率逆转所引发的资本流动变化。双变量异常检测方法能够更有效地识别金融市场压力异常时期，即股票与债券市场同时受到影响的时段。大多数被分类的异常事件与基金流动骤停（即股票与债券流动同时停止）或激增（即股票与债券流动同时大幅增加）相关。总体而言，运用机器学习技术的单变量与双变量异常检测能够发挥重要作用，有助于更深入地理解资本流动的骤停与激增现象。

<summary><strong>英文摘要</strong></summary>

This paper applies machine learning methods and anomaly detection to sudden stop analysis of portfolio flows. Using the isolation forest methodology, univariate as well as bivariate sudden stops of equity and bond fund flows to emerging markets are generated. An anomaly score and an anomaly classification are provided. The results point to an increase in anomalous portfolio flows to emerging markets in recent years. In addition, the isolation forest methodology appears to yield better results than the traditional approach to sudden stop analysis in classifying anomalies connected with the recent capital flow volatility related to the outbreak of the COVID-19 pandemic as well as the interest rate reversal in advanced economies in recent years. The bivariate approach to anomaly detection is better able to identify anomalous episodes of financial stress, where both equity and bond markets are simultaneously affected. Most of the classified anomalies are related to fund flow stops (i.e. simultaneous stops to both equity and bond flows) or surges (i.e. surges in both equity and bond flows). In general, univariate and bivariate anomaly detection using machine learning techniques can play an important part and lead to a better understanding of sudden stops and surges.
