<summary><strong>中文摘要</strong></summary>

Sai Ma、Viktors Stebunovs、Judit Temesvary 我们构建了一个“增长风险传导”（Transmission Growth-at-Risk, TGaR）框架，将外国金融脆弱性（foreign financial vulnerabilities）纳入美国下行增长风险的预测因子。我们区分了金融状况（financial conditions，衡量当前信贷市场的紧缩程度）与金融脆弱性（financial vulnerabilities，衡量可能放大冲击的结构性脆弱性）。外国金融脆弱性上升与美国增长风险（growth-at-risk）下降相关，其传导渠道包括贸易联系和美元一体化渠道（dollar integration channels）。海外资产估值压力（asset valuation pressures）和金融部门杠杆（financial sector leverage）具有最大的估计放大效应。金融状况主要影响短期尾部风险（tail risk），而外国脆弱性则在中期时间范围内对美国GDP构成压力。在样本外预测中，加入外国脆弱性指标后，8个季度时间范围内的预测得分提高了53%。危机时期的证据也指向相同的解释。这些发现表明，监测外国金融脆弱性对于评估美国增长前景具有重要意义。

<summary><strong>英文摘要</strong></summary>

Sai Ma , Viktors Stebunovs , Judit Temesvary We develop a Transmission Growth-at-Risk (TGaR) framework that incorporates foreign financial vulnerabilities as predictors of U.S. downside growth risk. We distinguish financial conditions, which measure current tightness in credit markets, from financial vulnerabilities, which measure structural fragilities that can amplify shocks. Elevated foreign financial vulnerabilities are associated with lower U.S. growth-at-risk, with transmission through both trade linkages and dollar integration channels. Asset valuation pressures and financial sector leverage abroad have the largest estimated amplification effects. Financial conditions primarily affect near-term tail risk, while foreign vulnerabilities weigh on U.S. GDP at a medium-term horizon. Out of sample, adding foreign vulnerabilities raises the predictive score by 53 percent at the 8-quarter horizon. Crisis-episode evidence points to the same interpretation. These findings show that monitoring foreign financial vulnerabilities is important for gauging U.S. growth prospects.
