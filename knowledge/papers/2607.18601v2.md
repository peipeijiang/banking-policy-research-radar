<summary><strong>中文摘要</strong></summary>

我们研究了用于估计溢出效应的实验的最优设计与分析。在已知（例如线性）暴露映射的假设下，我们刻画了能够最小化针对一大类不可观测变量分布的最坏情况渐近方差（worst-case asymptotic variance）的处理分配分布（treatment-assignment distribution）与基于回归的估计量（regression-based estimator）。该设计问题产生了一个直观的解决方案，其中规划者需要在溢出信号强度（spillover signal strength）与溢出变异扩散（diffusion of spillover variation）之间进行权衡。分析问题则产生了一个简单的重新中心化工具变量估计量（recentered instrumental variable estimator），以最佳地利用这种变异。该框架在若干基准情形（如聚类暴露，clustered exposure）中给出了自然的解决方案，并为一般网络（包括二分图设定，bipartite settings）提供了计算上易于处理的近似方法。我们基于发展经济学的两个应用案例，在半合成实验中展示了这些新工具。我们的方法在两个实验中都大幅降低了标准误，使有效样本量增加了50%-100%或更多。

<summary><strong>英文摘要</strong></summary>

We study the optimal design and analysis of experiments for estimating spillover effects. Assuming a known (e.g., linear) exposure mapping, we characterize the treatment-assignment distribution and regression-based estimator that minimize worst-case asymptotic variance against a broad class of distributions of unobservables. The design problem yields an intuitive solution in which the planner trades off spillover signal strength against diffusion of spillover variation. The analysis problem yields a simple recentered instrumental variable estimator to best leverage this variation. This framework produces natural solutions in several benchmark cases - such as clustered exposure - and suggests computationally tractable approximations for general networks, including bipartite settings. We illustrate these new tools in semi-synthetic experiments based on two applications from development economics. Our approach yields large standard error reductions in both experiments, increasing effective sample sizes by 50-100% or more.
