<summary><strong>中文摘要</strong></summary>

本工作论文对斯蒂芬·B·卡普兰（Stephen B. Kaplan）的《全球化与拉丁美洲的紧缩政治》（Globalization and Austerity Politics in Latin America）进行了分析性评述。文章考察了卡普兰提出的政治性紧缩周期（Political Austerity Cycle, PAC）框架，并探讨了拉丁美洲地区全球化、通货膨胀、选举激励与宏观经济政策制定之间的关系。论文评估了卡普兰的理论模型，回顾了其在拉丁美洲各国中的实证应用，并从国际政治经济学（International Political Economy）的视角对其优势与局限进行了批判性分析。文中特别关注了全球化、政治制度、通胀危机与财政政策之间的互动关系。本稿件最初于2015年作为国际政治经济学本科课程作业撰写，现作为学术工作论文保存，记录了作者在比较政治经济学、全球化及拉丁美洲经济治理方面的早期研究兴趣。本文的分享旨在促进学术交流与档案保存。

<summary><strong>英文摘要</strong></summary>

This working paper presents an analytical review of Stephen B. Kaplan's Globalization and Austerity Politics in Latin America. It examines the Political Austerity Cycle (PAC) framework proposed by Kaplan and discusses the relationship between globalization, inflation, electoral incentives, and macroeconomic policymaking in Latin America. The paper evaluates Kaplan's theoretical model, reviews its empirical application across Latin American countries, and offers a critical assessment of its strengths and limitations from the perspective of International Political Economy. Special attention is given to the interaction between globalization, political institutions, inflationary crises, and fiscal policy. Originally written in 2015 as part of undergraduate coursework in International Political Economy, this manuscript is preserved as an academic working paper documenting the author's early research interests in comparative political economy, globalization, and Latin American economic governance. It is shared for scholarly communication and archival purposes.
