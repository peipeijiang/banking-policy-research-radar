<summary><strong>中文摘要</strong></summary>

我们研究如何从匿名的总体信念数据中识别贝叶斯主体群体中的信念分布。尽管单个贝叶斯主体的完整信念可以通过其对一组适当的二元事件（binary events）的信念来恢复，但这一原理未必适用于群体：逐事件的信念分布可能无法识别潜在的先验分布。我们研究了这种失效是普遍存在还是仅在特殊情况下发生。识别能力取决于所观测的事件族在状态空间上诱导的图论结构。在$n$个主体的分布中，若诱导图是不可分离的（nonseparable），则识别是普遍的；若图是可分离的（separable），则非识别是普遍的。这些结果既确立了从总体信念数据中恢复信念异质性的局限性，也提供了相应的设计原则。

</details>

<details>
<summary><strong>英文摘要</strong></summary>

We study the identification of belief distributions in a population of Bayesian agents from anonymous aggregate belief data. While a single Bayesian agent's full belief can be recovered from beliefs over a suitable collection of binary events, this principle need not extend to populations: event-by-event distributions of beliefs may fail to identify the underlying distribution of priors. We study when this failure is generic and when it is exceptional. Identification is governed by the graph-theoretic structure induced by the observed family of events on the state space. Among $n$-agent distributions, identification is generic if the induced graph is nonseparable, while non-identification is generic if the graph is separable. The results establish both limits and design principles for recovering belief heterogeneity from aggregate belief data.

</details>
