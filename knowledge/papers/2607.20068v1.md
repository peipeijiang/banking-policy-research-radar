<summary><strong>中文摘要</strong></summary>

无论是新冠疫情还是伊朗战争，近期事件都凸显了供应链的系统性脆弱性。由于买卖双方之间高度特定且相互依赖的关系，即便单一企业的失败也可能引发供应链网络中上下游的级联失效，从而造成系统性的经济混乱。直到最近，量化单个企业失败对整个供应链的系统性影响才成为可能。在此，我们证明，多个企业同时失败所产生的系统性风险贡献，可能远大于这些企业单独失败所造成损害的总和。利用一组独特的数据集，我们得以在微观企业层面重建厄瓜多尔的国家供应链网络，并发现企业联合失败可产生高达257倍的系统性风险放大效应。然而，仅有0.14%的极小部分企业组合表现出超过4倍的系统性风险放大。我们开发了一种简单方法来识别导致大规模系统性风险放大的企业组合。这种放大的根源在于违约供应商的可替代性崩溃。我们讨论了这种罕见但强烈的系统性风险放大现象对同时影响多个企业（如自然灾害和战争）的情境的启示意义。

<summary><strong>英文摘要</strong></summary>

Whether the COVID-19 pandemic or the Iran war, recent events have highlighted the systemic fragility of supply chains. Due to highly specific and mutual buyer-supplier dependencies, even the failure of a single firm can cause system-wide economic disruptions in the form of cascading failures up and down the supply chain network. Only recently has it become possible to quantify the systemic impact of the failure of individual firms on the total supply chain. Here, we demonstrate that the systemic risk contributions of combinations of firm failures can be drastically larger than the sum of the damage caused by the firms individually. Using a unique data set that allows us to reconstruct the national supply chain network of Ecuador at the firm-level, we find that combined failures can produce systemic risk amplifications of up to a factor of 257. However, only a tiny fraction of 0.14\% of pairs exhibit a more than 4-fold amplification of systemic risk. We develop a simple method to identify firm combinations that lead to large systemic risk amplifications. The origin of these amplifications is a breakdown of the substitutability of defaulted suppliers. We discuss the implications of the existence of rare but strong systemic risk amplification for situations that simultaneously affect multiple firms, such as natural disasters and wars.
