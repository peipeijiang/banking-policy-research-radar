<summary><strong>中文摘要</strong></summary>

与教育程度相关的基因能够因果性地提高劳动力市场收入，但这一关系背后的经济机制尚不明确。利用英国生物银行（UK Biobank）中兄弟姐妹间遗传变异的准随机性，我估算了教育多基因指数（Education PolyGenic Index, Ed PGI）对教育程度和收入的因果效应。Ed PGI每增加一个标准差，可使完成的教育年限提高0.5年，并使晚年收入增加约5%（复现了Carvalho 2025年的主要估计结果）。随后，我采用因果中介分析框架，将这一总遗传收入效应分解为通过教育年限起作用的间接渠道和剩余的直接效应。与基于结构模型的分解方法不同，本方法基于研究设计；其中一项不确定性来源（额外一年教育的因果回报）通过敏感性分析得以透明处理。在教育回报率约为6%的相关性估计下，总遗传收入效应中有65%至75%通过教育年限渠道发挥作用。经济学文献中关于英国的准实验估计表明教育回报率更高，这意味着中介份额更大，且Ed PGI的大部分收入效应是通过完成的教育年限实现的。

<summary><strong>英文摘要</strong></summary>

Genes associated with educational attainment causally improve labour market income, but the economic mechanism behind this relationship is not clear. Using quasi-random variation in genetic inheritance across siblings in the UK Biobank, I estimate the causal effect of the Education PolyGenic Index (Ed PGI) on education and income. A one standard deviation increase in the Ed PGI raises completed education by 0.5 years and later-life income by around 5 percent (replicating the main estimates in Carvalho 2025). I then decompose this total genetic income effect into an indirect channel operating through education years and a residual direct effect, using a causal mediation framework. Unlike structural model-based decompositions, this approach is design-based; one remaining source of uncertainty (the causal return to an extra year of education) is handled transparently through a sensitivity analysis. At correlational education returns of around 6 percent, 65 to 75 percent of the total genetic income effect operates through the years of education channel. Quasi-experimental estimates from the economics literature for Britain imply higher returns to education, suggesting that the mediated share is larger and that the majority of the Ed PGI's income effect operates through completed years of education.
