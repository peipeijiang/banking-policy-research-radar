多梅尼科·费拉罗（Domenico Ferraro）、朱塞佩·菲奥里（Giuseppe Fiori）与达米安·皮耶里（Damian Pierri）构建了一个产品市场与劳动力市场均为完全竞争的商业周期模型。在该模型中，生产活动需要最低劳动投入，从而产生内生性产能利用率。总生产函数呈现折线形态：在产能以下（通常为经济衰退期）表现为规模报酬不变，而在扩张期达到产能上限时则呈现规模报酬递减。基于叙事识别法确认的劳动力税收冲击在产能利用率低于趋势水平时对工时与产出具有显著更强效应的新实证证据，我们利用美国数据对模型进行校准，结果表明经济衰退期的总工时弹性更高，这与偏好所隐含的微观弹性存在显著差异。

<summary><strong>英文摘要</strong></summary>

Domenico Ferraro, Giuseppe Fiori , and Damian Pierri We develop a business cycle model with perfectly competitive product and labor markets in which production requires a minimum labor input, generating endogenous capacity utilization. The aggregate production function is kinked, featuring constant returns to scale below capacity—typically in recessions—and decreasing returns at capacity in expansions. Motivated by new empirical evidence that narratively identified labor tax shocks have significantly larger effects on hours and output when capacity utilization is below trend, we calibrate the model to U.S. data and show that the aggregate hours elasticity is higher in recessions, differing markedly from the micro elasticity implied by preferences.
