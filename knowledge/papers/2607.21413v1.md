<summary><strong>中文摘要</strong></summary>

应用计量经济学家通常将每个个体建模为在实验组和对照组中具有固定结果，并在工具变量（IV）设定中，对工具的每个取值具有固定的处理决策。本文探讨当结果以及处理分配或选择在个体层面具有随机性时，会发生何种变化。在该模型中，每个个体具有一个稳定（但可能随机）的反应类型，该类型由两个要素构成：每种状态下的处理选择概率，以及每种处理-状态配对下的潜在结果分布。这些随机潜在结果改变了一些常见估计量的解释。例如，在确定性IV模型中，估计目标仅识别出依从者——即那些处理状态随工具变化而改变的个体——的处理效应。在随机处理分配或选择下，不存在这样的子群体：估计目标对所有个体的效应进行平均，并根据工具、政策或分配规则改变其处理概率的程度对每个个体进行加权。随后，本文为随机选择提供了一个基于信息的基础，在该基础中，个体根据其信息所获得的预期收益采取行动。

<summary><strong>英文摘要</strong></summary>

Applied econometricians typically model each individual as having fixed outcomes under treatment and control and, in instrumental-variables (IV) settings, fixed treatment decisions under each value of the instrument. This paper asks what changes when outcomes and treatment allocations or choices are stochastic at the individual level. In the model, each individual has a stable (but possibly stochastic) response type consisting of two objects: a treatment choice probability under each state and a potential outcome distribution under each treatment-state pair. These stochastic potential outcomes change the interpretation of some familiar estimators. For instance, in the deterministic IV model, the estimand identifies treatment effect only for compliers - those whose treatment status switches with the instrument. Under stochastic treatment allocation or choice there is no such subgroup: the estimand averages effects over all individuals, weighting each by how much the instrument, policy, or assignment rule moves their probability of treatment. The paper then gives an information-based foundation for stochastic choice, in which individuals act on expected gains given their information.
