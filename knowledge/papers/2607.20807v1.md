<summary><strong>中文摘要</strong></summary>

人工智能在执行任务上比在评估任务上更容易实现自动化：产生输出成本低廉，而判断其正确性则代价高昂。暴露度指标根据人工智能能否执行任务来对工作进行排序，而非依据人类提供何种功能。我按照固定评分标准对全部19,265条O*NET任务陈述进行评分，以构建职业层面的执行份额与人工智能能力份额。该执行份额在不同模型编码者及O*NET版本间具有可复现性，且与人工智能能力及常规任务强度存在显著差异；它是一种基于模型的度量指标，而非经人类验证的基准事实，并且相较于O*NET的评估活动，其增量解释力有限。在协调面板数据中，自2012年以来，每个时间窗口内执行密集型白领职业的就业增长均较低，且斜率相等的原假设无法被拒绝：该梯度是一种长期趋势而非人工智能时代的特定事件，且主要存在于职业大类之间。基于版本更新的能力梯度在2022年后趋于陡峭，这一变化具有时间标记但无法归因于因果关系。本证据建立了一种度量指标与时间序列，而非人工智能引发的效应。

<summary><strong>英文摘要</strong></summary>

Artificial intelligence automates execution more readily than evaluation: producing output is cheap, judging whether it is correct is not. Exposure measures rank tasks by whether AI can perform them, not by which function the human supplies. I score all $19{,}265$ O*NET task statements under fixed rubrics to build occupation-level execution and AI-capability shares. The execution share is reproducible across model coders and O*NET vintages and distinct from AI capability and routine-task intensity; it is a model-based measure, not human-validated ground truth, and adds only modest power beyond O*NET's evaluation activities. In a harmonized panel, employment growth is lower in execution-heavy white-collar occupations in every window since 2012, and equality of slopes cannot be rejected: the gradient is a secular trend rather than an AI-era event, largely between occupational families. The vintage-valid capability gradient steepens after 2022, a change that is dated but not causally attributable. The evidence establishes a measure and a chronology, not an AI-caused effect.
