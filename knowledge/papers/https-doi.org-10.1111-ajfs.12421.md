<summary><strong>中文摘要</strong></summary>

本研究探讨了韩国公司债券市场中流动性风险的定价问题。我们采用了三种不同的流动性因子——即总体市场流动性（aggregate market liquidity）、流动性创新（liquidity innovation）以及预测流动性（predicted liquidity）。实证结果表明，虽然以市场流动性因子衡量时韩国公司债券市场存在流动性溢价（liquidity premium），但以预测流动性因子衡量时却出现了流动性折价（liquidity discount）。借鉴先前的研究，我们进一步指出，对非预期流动性冲击（unexpected liquidity shocks）具有高敏感性的投资组合之所以获得较低（较高）收益，可能归因于韩国市场中AAA级（AAA）与A级（A）债券交易频率的差异（即低频与高频交易）。最后，我们的发现表明，尽管预期中存在流动性溢价，但在韩国公司债券市场中，投资者因承担预测流动性风险（predicted liquidity risk）而受到了惩罚。

<summary><strong>英文摘要</strong></summary>

Abstract This study investigates the pricing of liquidity risk in the Korean corporate bond market. We use three different liquidity factors — namely, aggregate market liquidity, liquidity innovation, and predicted liquidity. The empirical results show that, while a liquidity premium exists in the Korean corporate bond market when measured by the market liquidity factor, a liquidity discount occurs when measured by the predicted liquidity factor. Drawing on prior studies, we further describe that the lower (higher) returns for portfolios with a high sensitivity to unexpected liquidity shocks may be attributable to the infrequent (frequent) trading of AAA(A)‐rated bonds in the Korean market. Finally, our findings suggest that while a liquidity premium exists in expectation, investors are penalized for taking predicted liquidity risks in the Korean corporate bond market.
