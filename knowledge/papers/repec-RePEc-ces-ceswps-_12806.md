<summary><strong>中文摘要</strong></summary>

本文研究了经济政策不确定性（Economic Policy Uncertainty, EPU）如何影响2010年1月至2020年12月期间448只标普500（S&P 500）股票的收益率与波动率（以平方收益率作为代理指标），并探讨了波动率持续性是否与EPU敏感性相关。持续性通过三种半参数分数积分阶数估计量进行度量：Geweke–Porter-Hudak对数周期图回归、局部Whittle估计以及精确局部Whittle估计。线性EPU–波动率关系在市场层面控制变量下不再显著，但极端EPU冲击会导致约五分之一样本股票产生显著的波动率响应，其中负面（不利）消息的影响略大。波动率的长期记忆性普遍存在。在标准带宽下，持续性与EPU敏感性呈负相关（ρ = −0.182, p = 0.0001），这一关系在控制了规模、流动性、市场贝塔系数及行业固定效应后依然成立，并且对于根据Qu（2011）检验其长期记忆性具有统计显著性的股票子样本同样成立。该关联主要集中在低频部分，与可能由水平位移引起的低频现象一致。

<summary><strong>英文摘要</strong></summary>

This paper investigates how Economic Policy Uncertainty (EPU) affects the returns and volatility (proxied by squared returns) of 448 S&P 500 stocks over the period January 2010–December 2020, and whether volatility persistence is related to EPU sensitivity. Persistence is measured with three semiparametric estimators of the degree of fractional integration: the Geweke–Porter-Hudak log-periodogram regression, the local Whittle, and the exact local Whittle. The linear EPU–volatility link does not survive market-level controls, but extreme EPU shocks generate significant volatility responses in roughly one-fifth of the stocks examined, with a slightly greater impact of adverse (bad) news. Long memory in volatility is pervasive. Persistence and EPU sensitivity are negatively related at the standard bandwidth (ρ = −0.182, p = 0.0001), a link that survives controls for size, liquidity, market beta, and sector fixed effects, and that holds for the subsample of stocks whose long memory is statistically significant according to the Qu (2011) test. This association is mainly concentrated at low frequencies, consistently with a low-frequency phenomenon possibly caused by level shifts.
