<summary><strong>中文摘要</strong></summary>

人工智能工具已大幅降低了生成提案、论文和计划的成本。本文认为，其结果并非创造性加速，而是提案膨胀：一种纳什均衡（Nash equilibrium）状态，在此状态下，提案成本低廉且地位崇高，而交付（shipping）成本高昂且失败可见。我们利用相空间（phase space）中的双吸引子系统对此建模，其中“思考者吸引子”（Thinker attractor）将智能体困于模拟之中，而“践行者吸引子”（Thinkerer attractor）则通过迭代交付迫使其与现实接触。以塔莎·亚尔（Tasha Yar）拒绝离开全息甲板（holodeck）作为结构性隐喻，我们分析了人工智能辅助提案写作的多巴胺机制、同行评审的强化作用，以及延迟迭代所带来的滞后学习成本。我们将此与2023年美国编剧工会（WGA）/美国电视与广播艺人联合会（SAG-AFTRA）编剧罢工进行对比，将其视为人类集体拒绝停留在模拟中的罕见案例。我们提出了一套基于技术成熟度等级（TRL）的评估标准、一组反瘫痪工作坊活动、针对思考者型认知特征的诊断方法，以及防止投机取巧的防护措施。唯有当交付比停滞成本更低时——这取决于结构性设计，而非个人意志——才能实现从全息甲板中的逃离。

<summary><strong>英文摘要</strong></summary>

AI tools have dramatically lowered the cost of generating proposals, papers, and plans. This paper argues that the result is not creative acceleration — it is proposal inflation: a Nash equilibrium in which proposing is low-cost and high-status while shipping remains high-cost and failure-visible. We model this using a dual-attractor system in phase space, with the Thinker attractor trapping agents in simulation and the Thinkerer attractor forcing contact with reality through iterative shipping. Using Tasha Yar's refusal to leave the holodeck as a structural metaphor, we analyze the dopamine mechanics of AI-assisted proposal writing, the reinforcing role of peer review, and the delayed learning cost of deferred iteration. We contrast this with the 2023 WGA/SAG-AFTRA writers' strike as a rare instance of humans collectively refusing to stay in the sim. We propose a TRL-graded assessment rubric, a set of anti-paralysis workshop activities, diagnostics for Thinker-type cognitive profiles, and guardrails against gaming. Escape from the holodeck occurs only when shipping becomes cheaper than stalling — by structural design, not individual willpower.

</details>
