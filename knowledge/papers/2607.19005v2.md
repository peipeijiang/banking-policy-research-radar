<summary><strong>中文摘要</strong></summary>

可观测矩阵动力学（Observable Matrix Dynamics, OMD）方法通过固定大小距离矩阵及其谱的轨迹来监测复杂非线性系统的时间演化。我们将其应用于三个危机十年期间的标普500指数（S&P 500）截面数据，即2001年互联网泡沫破裂、2007—2008年金融危机以及2020年新冠疫情崩盘，并在固定宇宙上选取三个固定大小的可观测变量。滚动收益率反余弦距离矩阵（arccos distance matrix）解读了相关几何结构：其有效维度在2008年和2020年危机期间发生坍缩，而2001年泡沫破裂则表现为分散式平仓。与机器学习距离矩阵对比，其谱始终处于未松弛的预学习状态，不存在低维流形，因此市场从未学习其相关结构或松弛至平稳几何形态。扣除市场因子后，可揭示出连贯的板块轮动，其名称级归因能够识别哪些股票驱动了每次危机及其驱动顺序。在短回溯窗口下，这些信号可解析出前兆并预测内生的2008年危机，但无法预测外生的2020年冲击。另外两个可观测变量将日收益率和波动率排名建模为其排名空间上的马尔可夫链（Markov chain）。收益率链具有由防御性板块主导的持续领头羊及近乎可逆的动力学特征。波动率链的持续性远强于前者，由金融板块主导，且是唯一携带微弱、偶发性时间箭头（arrow of time）的变量，该时间箭头在市场压力期爆发，与波动率聚集效应及Zumbach效应（Zumbach effect）相匹配。所有三个矩阵在市场崩盘期间均呈现出一致的变化。

<summary><strong>英文摘要</strong></summary>

The Observable Matrix Dynamics (OMD) approach monitors the time development of complex non-linear systems through the trajectory of a fixed-size distance matrix and its spectrum. We apply it to the S\&P 500 cross section over three crisis decades, the 2001 dot-com bust, the 2007--2008 financial crisis, and the 2020 Covid crash, with three fixed-size observables on a fixed universe. The arccos distance matrix of the rolling return correlations reads the correlation geometry: its effective dimension collapses at the 2008 and 2020 crises, while the 2001 bust is a dispersed unwind. Read against machine-learning distance matrices, its spectrum stays in the un-relaxed, pre-learning regime with no low-dimensional manifold, so the market never learns its correlation structure or relaxes to a stationary geometry. Subtracting the market factor exposes a coherent sector rotation, whose name-level attribution identifies which stocks drive each crisis and in what order. At a short lookback these signals resolve precursors and forecast the endogenous 2008 crisis, though not the exogenous 2020 shock. The other two observables model the daily return and volatility rankings as Markov chains on their ranking spaces. The return chain has persistent, defensive-led bellwethers and near-reversible dynamics. The volatility chain is far more persistent, led by the financial sector, and is the only one to carry a weak, episodic arrow of time, flaring at market stress and matching volatility clustering and the Zumbach effect. All three matrices show coherent changes during market crashes.
