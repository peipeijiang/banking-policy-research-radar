<summary><strong>中文摘要</strong></summary>

实验者经常进行预实验，但小规模预实验应在多大程度上影响主实验设计尚无定论。本文展示了在两波实验中，有噪声的预实验证据应如何指导处理分配概率。两种经典规则代表了极端情况。平衡分配可防范最坏情况，但忽略了某一实验组噪声更大的证据。可行的内曼分配具有适应性，但在预实验样本有限时可能对噪声反应过度，导致任意大的精度损失。我们提出一种条件极小化极大遗憾（Conditional Minimax Regret, CMR）规则，该规则在针对处理组和对照组方差构建的有限样本置信集上最小化最坏情况遗憾。CMR以高概率保留平衡分配的最坏情况保护能力，随着预实验规模增大而收敛至内曼分配，并以常数倍达到极小化极大遗憾率。该方法可扩展至多臂和分层设计，基于四项实地实验校准的模拟表明，它避免了可行内曼分配在预实验规模较小时产生的严重损失，同时捕获了其在预实验规模较大时的大部分收益。

<summary><strong>英文摘要</strong></summary>

Experimenters often run pilots, but how much a small pilot should shape the main-wave design has no settled answer. This paper shows how noisy pilot evidence should guide treatment assignment probabilities in two-wave experiments. Two canonical rules mark the extremes. Balanced assignment guards against worst cases but ignores evidence that one arm is noisier. Feasible Neyman allocation adapts, but with a finite pilot it can overreact to noise, producing arbitrarily large precision losses. We propose a Conditional Minimax Regret (CMR) rule that minimizes worst-case regret over a finite-sample confidence set for the treatment and control variances. CMR retains balance's worst-case protection with high probability, converges to the Neyman allocation as the pilot grows, and attains the minimax-regret rate up to constants. It extends to multi-arm and stratified designs, and simulations calibrated to four field experiments show it avoids feasible Neyman's severe small-pilot losses while capturing most of its large-pilot gains.
