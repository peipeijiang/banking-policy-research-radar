<summary><strong>中文摘要</strong></summary>

应对非法资金流动的策略随着全球化的发展而演变，这给发展中国家在遵循国际合规规范方面带来了严峻挑战。本研究评估了全球化如何调节斯里兰卡持牌商业银行部门的反洗钱（Anti-Money Laundering, AML）与打击恐怖主义融资（Countering the Financing of Terrorism, CFT）策略。通过收集150名风险管理专家的调查反馈，研究采用主成分分析和多元回归方法，深入分析了全球一体化所带来的立法、技术和制度性挑战。研究结果显示，银行业专业人士认为机构对反洗钱/打击恐怖主义融资（AML/CFT）合规具有坚定承诺，并对现有框架持积极态度。交易监控系统与培训/意识被确认为降低洗钱和恐怖主义融资风险的关键因素。本研究强调了加强基于风险的方法、强化尽职调查、促进全球合作以及持续投资于能力建设的重要性，以确保在全球化环境下构建具有韧性的金融犯罪预防体系。

<summary><strong>英文摘要</strong></summary>

Strategies for combating illicit financial flows have evolved with globalisation, presenting serious challenges for developing countries navigating international compliance norms. This study provides an evaluation of how globalisation moderates Anti-Money Laundering (AML) and strategies for Countering the Financing of Terrorism (CFT) in Sri Lanka’s licensed commercial banking sector. Utilising responses from 150 risk management specialists, the research uses principal component analysis and multiple regression to yield insights into the legislative, technical, and institutional challenges posed by global integration. Findings reveal that banking professionals perceive strong institutional commitment to AML/CFT compliance and hold positive views on existing frameworks. Transaction Monitoring Systems and Training/Awareness emerge as critical factors in reducing money laundering and terrorism financing risks. The study underscores the necessity of strengthening risk-based approaches, enhancing due diligence, fostering global collaboration, and investing in continuous capacity building to ensure resilient financial crime prevention systems in a globalised environment.
