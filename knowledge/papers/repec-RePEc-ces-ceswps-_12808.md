# Equilibrium Excess Demand in the Low Income Rental Housing Market: Theory and Experimental Evidence

<summary><strong>中文摘要</strong></summary>

我们构建了一个针对低收入家庭租赁住房市场的垄断竞争模型，其中包含内生性租约违约。同质化的供应商决定租赁单元的供给数量及租金价格，以最大化预期利润。潜在租户的收入存在差异，并面临不可保的收入风险，他们需选择是否进行高成本的租赁住房搜寻。若搜寻并找到租赁单元，则必须在收入不确定性消除前签订租约。因此，部分租户可能发生租金违约。研究表明，在无任何政府价格管制的情况下，租约违约可解释低收入租赁住房市场中持续存在的超额需求，且此类超额需求可能导致政府管制产生非标准效应。我们还通过设计实验室实验检验超额需求均衡的行为合理性。实验结果显示，在反馈与重复机制作用下，要求房东参与者限制供给的超额需求均衡，其达成难度并不高于市场出清均衡。

<summary><strong>英文摘要</strong></summary>

We develop a model of monopolistic competition in the rental housing market for low-income households with endogenous tenancy default. Identical suppliers choose the number of rental units to supply and the rental price to charge to maximize expected profits. Potential tenants who differ in their incomes and face an uninsurable income risk choose whether to engage in a costly search for rental housing. If they search and find a rental unit, then they must commit to a rental agreement before their income uncertainty is resolved. Consequently, some tenants may default on their rental payments. We show that tenancy default can explain persistent excess demand in the low-income rental housing market without any government price regulations, and that such excess demand can lead to nonstandard effects of government regulations. We also test whether the excess-demand equilibrium is behaviorally plausible by designing a laboratory experiment. Our experimental results reveal that, with feedback and repetition, the excess-demand equilibrium that requires landlord participants to restrict supply is no more difficult for participants to reach than a market-clearing equilibrium.
