<summary><strong>英文摘要</strong></summary>

We present a resolution of the cusp singularity y² = x³ at the origin using the RICIS-III framework.Instead of classical blow-up, which introduces a projective line and increases local dimension, we apply the structural algebra of typed zeros 0_F := F × 0 (Axiom A10). The singularity is resolved via the structural relation 0_{(y²)} / 0_{(x³)} = y²/x³, which reduces to t² under the parametrization y ~ t x^{3/2} and the Law of Absolute Identity (L1). The result t² = x coincides with the classical strict transform, but is achieved without dimensional inflation, without projective extensions, and without loss of provenance. We also address a critical objection: in the quotient ring k[[x,y]]/(y²-x³), the elements y² and x³ are equal in value, yet their typed zeros remain structurally distinct because RICIS-III compares Expression Trees, not quotient values.
