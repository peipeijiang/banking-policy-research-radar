从家庭视角审视噪声污染的经济影响，是环境可持续性研究中的一个新兴领域。经济学研究涵盖噪声污染的来源、测度、后果，以及用于识别噪声污染对社会经济福利因果影响的计量经济学方法。随着工业增长与城市发展，噪声源大致可分为四类：机场、铁路、城市交通及邻里社区。经济学研究中通常采用四类测度或数据来源来捕捉噪声变化，即：与噪声源的邻近程度、实时噪声监测记录、家庭调查以及噪声投诉行政记录。噪声污染的社会经济后果涵盖从身心健康到幸福感、暴力与自杀、住房市场资本化及不平等现象。在经济学研究中，通常运用三类计量经济学方法来识别噪声污染对家庭福利的因果影响，即工具变量估计、双重差分估计以及随机与准自然实验。基于经济学研究得出的噪声污染对家庭社会经济福利的因果影响，有助于指导政策制定中噪声消除资源的分配，并进行成本效益分析。经济学研究从概念与方法论两个层面为一般性噪声研究做出贡献：它将研究范围从隔音技术或场地布局规划拓展至人类福祉，并致力于从其他混杂因素中分离出噪声污染的因果影响。未来，随着数据可得性的提高，有必要围绕噪声污染的环境不公正问题及其在欠发达国家中的社会经济后果展开进一步研究。

<summary><strong>英文摘要</strong></summary>

Examining the economic impact of noise pollution from a lens of household is a burgeoning field in the study of environmental sustainability. Economics studies cover the source, measure, consequence of noise pollution, as well as the econometric methods used to identify the causal impact of noise pollution on socioeconomic welfare. There are broadly four major noise origins along with the industrial growth and urban development, which are airport, railway, urban traffic, and neighborhood. Four general kinds of measures or data sources are used in economics studies to capture the noise variations, namely, proximity to noise origins, real-time noise monitor records, household surveys, and administrative records on noise complaints. The socioeconomic consequences of noise pollution span from physical or mental health to happiness, violence and suicide, housing market capitalization, and inequality. In economics studies, generally three types of econometric methods are used to identify causal impact of noise pollution on the household's welfare, which are instrumental variable estimation, difference-in-difference estimation, randomized and quasi-natural experiments. The causal impact of noise pollution on household's socioeconomic welfare derived from economics studies can help guide policy efforts in allocating resources for noise elimination and conduct cost-benefit analysis. The economics research contributes to the general noise research from both conceptual and methodological perspectives: It expands the scope of research from sound-poof technology or site layout planning to human welfare, and endeavors to isolate the causal impact of noise pollution from other confounding factors. Future studies are warranted along the lines of environmental injustice of noise pollution and socioeconomic consequences in less developed countries when the data become more available.
