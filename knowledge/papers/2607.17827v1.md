<summary><strong>中文摘要</strong></summary>

一个集体旅游品牌由不同管理者所管理的资产共同生产，因此目的地管理者必须决定哪些资产需要集体保护。我们将一个目的地建模为一个“门”（gate）乘以一个“菜单”（menu）。门将关键性投入与不可复制的锚点（anchors）组合为一种弱关联公共品（weaker-link public good）；菜单则通过多样性偏好将众多可复制的景点组合在一起。约束性门要素的恶化会产生持续的、波及整个品牌的影响，而单个菜单景点的份额则随菜单增长而逐渐消失。Aumann-Shapley会计方法将共同生产的品牌价值分配到各项资产上。在给定的剩余价值与非使用价值条件下，分散化的管理者在保持其他品质不变时，会倾向于对物理门质量维护不足。我们利用两个语言模型，从涵盖166个经济体的导游文本中测量该框架的构念，并将门标签与世界遗产名录进行部分外部校验，同时以四个法国葡萄酒产区为例，说明品牌内部污染排序。

</details>

<summary><strong>英文摘要</strong></summary>

A collective tourism brand is jointly produced by assets managed by different custodians, so destination managers must decide which assets require collective protection. We model a destination as a gate times a menu. The gate combines essential inputs and non-reproducible anchors as a weaker-link public good; the menu combines many reproducible attractions through love of variety. Deterioration of a binding gate element has a non-vanishing, brand-wide effect, whereas an individual menu attraction's share vanishes as the menu grows. Aumann-Shapley accounting allocates the jointly produced brand value across assets. Under stated surplus and non-use-value conditions, a decentralized custodian weakly under-maintains physical gate quality holding other qualities fixed. We measure the framework's constructs from guide text with two language models across 166 economies, subject the gate labels to a partial external check against the World Heritage List, and illustrate a within-brand contamination ordering with four French wine regions.

</details>
