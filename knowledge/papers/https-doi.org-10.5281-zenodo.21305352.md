<summary><strong>中文摘要</strong></summary>

在全球多极化背景下，以美元为主导的跨境金融体系使西方大国得以部署两种差异化的单边遏制工具：冻结主权外汇储备以及对政治精英的海外私人资产实施司法扣押。现有学术研究倾向于将地缘政治联盟、输入型通胀、美元制裁、货币稳定与以人民为中心的生计保障等议题分门别类地加以探讨，缺乏一条将长期制度性结构缺陷、叠加的外部金融约束、扭曲的国内政策安排、实物货币锚的侵蚀以及宏观经济指标与基层民生感知之间的持续背离相联结的整合性分析链条。本文构建了一个统一的理论框架，融合了依附理论、分层实物货币锚理论、货币主权理论、资源诅咒理论、后发优势理论以及以人民为中心的可持续发展范式。本文严格区分了良性比较优势贸易分工与对生存必需品的结构性过度依赖、正式制度文本与有缺陷的执行机制、高端制造业带来的短期外汇收益与基于生计的长期实物产出支撑。以2022年至2024年的菲律宾为核心研究案例，本研究依赖菲律宾统计局、亚洲开发银行、联合国粮农组织和国际货币基金组织发布的可验证官方时序数据，进行客观的机制分析，而不对政策制定者的主观意图或未来政治命运进行推测。本文引入了梯度比较案例，包括索马里的系统性货币崩溃和斯里兰卡的主权债务危机作为负面危机样本，俄罗斯的自主去美元化实践作为正面突破样本，越南和印度尼西亚的温和多元化改革作为过渡性平衡样本，以及日韩高端制造业经济体作为过渡性风险样本。研究量化了对基本民生必需品过度依赖的风险阈值，并梳理了冷战时期与当代美国代理人遏制策略的演变逻辑。研究结论表明，产业、粮食安全、统计监管、外交和货币治理领域的长期制度缺陷构成了治理扭曲的根本结构性根源，而海外资产司法控制与全球输入型通胀仅扮演了风险放大器的角色。一国货币的稳定购买力从根本上由其国内自主生产的粮食、能源及其他民生必需品所支撑，而非由高科技出口所产生的短期外汇红利所决定。受制于技术瓶颈与后发追赶效应，高端产业的利润优势仅是暂时的缓冲，无法化解由外部供给依赖所导致的长期结构性脆弱。对于大多数发展中边缘经济体而言，完善制度体系以保障核心民生必需品的自主供给，是削弱外部单边约束、实现以人民为中心的可持续发展的结构性路径。

<summary><strong>英文摘要</strong></summary>

Abstract Against the backdrop of global multipolarization, dollar-dominated cross-border financial systems have enabled Western powers to deploy two differentiated unilateral containment tools: the freezing of sovereign foreign exchange reserves and the judicial seizure of overseas private assets belonging to political elites. Existing scholarship tends to compartmentalize research on geopolitical alliances, imported inflation, dollar sanctions, currency stability and people-centered livelihood security, lacking an integrated analytical chain linking long-term institutional structural defects, superimposed external financial restraints, distorted domestic policy arrangements, erosion of physical monetary anchors, and persistent divergence between macroeconomic indicators and grassroots livelihood perceptions. This paper constructs a unified theoretical framework integrating Dependency Theory, layered physical monetary anchor theory, monetary sovereignty theory, resource curse theory, latecomer advantage theory and the people-centered sustainable development paradigm. It strictly distinguishes benign comparative advantage trade division from structural over-reliance on survival necessities, formal institutional texts from defective enforcement mechanisms, and short-term foreign exchange gains from high-end manufacturing versus long-term livelihood-based physical output support. Taking the Philippines from 2022 to 2024 as the core research case, this study relies on verifiable official sequential data released by the Philippine Statistics Authority, ADB, FAO and IMF to conduct objective mechanism analysis without speculating on the subjective intentions or future political fate of policymakers. Gradient comparative cases are introduced, including Somalias systemic monetary collapse and Sri Lankas sovereign debt crisis as negative crisis samples, Russias autonomous de-dollarization practice as a positive breakthrough sample, Vietnam and Indonesias moderate diversified reform as transitional balanced samples, and Japan-South Korea high-endmanufacturing economies as transitional risk samples. The research quantifies risk thresholds for over-reliance on essential livelihood goods and sorts out the evolutionary logic of U.S. proxy containment strategies during the Cold War and the contemporary era. The study concludes that long-term institutional flaws in industry, food security, statistical supervision, diplomacy and monetary governance form the fundamental structural root of governance distortion, while overseas asset judicial control and global imported inflation merely act as risk amplifiers. The stable purchasing power of a countrys currency is fundamentally supported by domestically self-produced food, energy and other livelihood necessities rather than short-term foreign exchange dividends generated by high-tech exports. Constrained by technological bottlenecks and latecomer catch-up effects, the profit advantages of high-end industries are only temporary buffers and cannot resolve long-term structural fragility caused by external supply dependence. For most developing peripheral economies, improving institutional systems to guarantee independent supply of core livelihood necessities is a structural path to weaken unilateral external restraints and implement people-centered sustainable development. Keywords Dollar Financial Hegemony; Physical Monetary Anchor; Monetary Sovereignty; Dependency Theory; Autonomy of Basic Livelihood Supplies; Peripheral Economies; Governance Distortion
