<summary><strong>中文摘要</strong></summary>

我们构建了一个中观计量经济学框架，用以量化针对家庭杠杆的政策所产生的宏观经济效应。我们聚焦于基于借款人的宏观审慎措施（BBMs），这些措施在贷款发放环节对放贷标准进行监管。该方法首先在结构向量自回归-工具变量（structural VAR-IV）框架下识别放贷标准冲击，随后利用借款人对BBMs的异质性反应来分离这些冲击中由政策引发的成分。我们将该框架应用于法国——该国于2019年引入了偿债收入比（DSTI）上限和贷款期限上限。研究表明，放贷标准冲击对住房和信贷动态具有持久且具有经济意义的显著影响。相比之下，由BBMs引发的成分虽具有统计显著性，但其经济影响较为温和。在信贷市场中，该措施使借款利率上升约0.15至0.20个百分点，并在2022年至2023年间使实际住房信贷余额增速放缓至多0.8个百分点——在此期间，未偿信贷下降了多达8%，主要归因于利率上升。该措施还使房价增速在低谷时期降低了2至3个百分点。我们未发现其对实际住宅投资、家庭收入或实际国内生产总值（GDP）产生显著影响。这些结果表明，BBMs主要影响住房信贷市场中风险最高的部分，而不会产生广泛的宏观经济溢出效应，凸显了其作为增强金融韧性的针对性工具的作用。

<summary><strong>英文摘要</strong></summary>

We develop a mesoeconometric framework to quantify the macroeconomic effects of policies that target household leverage. We focus on borrower-based macroprudential measures (BBMs), which regulate lending standards at loan origination. The approach first identifies lending standards shocks in a structural VAR-IV framework and then exploits heterogeneous borrower responses to BBMs to isolate the policy-induced component of those shocks. We apply this framework to France, where caps on debt-service-to-income (DSTI) ratios and loan maturities were introduced in 2019. We show that lending standards shocks have persistent and economically meaningful effects on housing and credit dynamics. By contrast, the BBM-induced component has statistically significant but economically moderate effects. In credit markets, the measure raises borrowing interest rates by around 0.15 to 0.20 percentage points and slows the growth of real outstanding housing credit by up to 0.8 percentage points between 2022 and 2023, a period during which outstanding credit fell by as much as 8%, mainly as a result of higher interest rates. The measure also reduces house price growth by 2 to 3 percentage points at its trough. We find no significant impact on real residential investment, household income, or real GDP. These results indicate that BBMs primarily affect the riskiest component of the housing credit market without generating broad macroeconomic spillovers, highlighting their role as targeted tools for enhancing financial resilience.
