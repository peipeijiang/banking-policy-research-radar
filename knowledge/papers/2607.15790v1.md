<summary><strong>中文摘要</strong></summary>

在随机期望效用理论（Gul and Pesendorfer, 2006）中，偏好的分布可从随机选择中唯一识别。本文探讨这种识别性是否能够超越期望效用理论的范畴。我们首先证明，当风险偏好符合Gul（1991）的失望厌恶模型时，偏好的分布仍可被唯一识别。为评估这一结论的适用范围，我们随后考察了其他非期望效用模型。在更广泛的中位性偏好（betweenness preferences）类别（Dekel, 1986）中，随机效用可能无法被识别。若偏好局限于加权期望效用类（weighted expected utility class）（Chew, 1983），则呈现出更为细致的情形：在三种奖品设定下唯一识别成立，但在四种或更多奖品设定下则失效。这些发现表明，随机期望效用的唯一性特征可能延续至期望效用之外，但其延续性关键取决于所考虑的风险偏好类别。

<summary><strong>英文摘要</strong></summary>

In random expected utility (Gul and Pesendorfer, 2006), the distribution of preferences is uniquely identified from random choice. This paper investigates whether such identification extends beyond expected utility. We first show that when risk preferences conform to the disappointment aversion model of Gul (1991), the distribution of preferences remains uniquely identified. To assess the scope of this result, we then examine other models of non-expected utility. Within the broader class of betweenness preferences (Dekel, 1986), random utility can be unidentifiable. If preferences are confined to the weighted expected utility class (Chew, 1983), a more nuanced picture emerges: unique identification holds in a three-prize setting but fails with four or more prizes. These findings show that the uniqueness property of random expected utility may persist beyond expected utility, but its persistence critically depends on the class of risk preferences under consideration.
