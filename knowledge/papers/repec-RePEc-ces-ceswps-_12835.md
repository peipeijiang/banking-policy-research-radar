<summary><strong>中文摘要</strong></summary>

通货膨胀冲击对不同劳动者的影响并不相同。通过对比男性和女性，我们揭示出两个事实。第一，无论是需求驱动型还是供给驱动型的通货膨胀冲击，都会扩大行业和职业内部的性别工资差距。由于这两类冲击意味着相反的经济周期状况，其共同效应表明通货膨胀是相关的驱动因素。第二，女性和男性对相同通货膨胀冲击的劳动力市场后果感知存在差异：女性预期状况会恶化，而男性则预期状况会改善。这种不对称性形成了一种预期渠道，将通货膨胀与不平等的工资增长联系起来。我们在一个包含男性和女性劳动者、不完全信息以及性别特定模糊态度（ambiguity attitudes）的新凯恩斯主义搜寻与匹配模型（New Keynesian search-and-matching model）中，对这一渠道进行了形式化分析。共同的宏观经济信号使得厌恶模糊（ambiguity-averse）的女性更倾向于关注不利的供给驱动型状态。对于通货膨胀信号而言，这降低了她们相对于男性所感知的外部选择（outside options）和工资诉求。差异化的感知将共同的通货膨胀冲击转化为差异化的薪酬结果。

</details>

<summary><strong>英文摘要</strong></summary>

Inflationary shocks do not affect all workers equally. Comparing men and women, we document two facts. First, both demand- and supply-driven inflationary shocks widen the gender wage gap within industries and occupations. Because these shocks imply opposite business-cycle conditions, their common effect points to inflation as the relevant force. Second, women and men perceive the same inflationary shocks as having different labor-market consequences: women expect conditions to worsen, while men expect them to improve. This asymmetry creates an expectations channel linking inflation to unequal wage growth. We formalize this channel in a New Keynesian search-and-matching model with male and female workers, imperfect information, and gender-specific ambiguity attitudes. Common macroeconomic signals lead ambiguity-averse women to place greater weight on adverse supply-driven states. For inflationary signals, this lowers their perceived outside options and wage demands relative to men's. Divergent perceptions translate common inflationary shocks into divergent pay.

</details>
